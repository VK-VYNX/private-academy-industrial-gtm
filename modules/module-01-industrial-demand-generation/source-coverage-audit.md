Canonical upstream source:

`master-industrial-gtm-intelligence/master-intelligence/22-master-concept-index.md`

## Purpose

This file answers four questions:

1. Which master industrial GTM concepts are deeply taught in Module 1?
2. Which concepts are only partially covered?
3. Which concepts are referenced but not trained as a primary skill?
4. Which concepts should be reserved for later academy modules?

The goal is not to claim total extraction. The goal is to make the learning boundary visible so Module 1 remains focused, honest, and expandable.

## Coverage Definitions

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Deeply Covered | The concept is explained, operationalized, exercised, critiqued, translated into RevOps or cadence implications, and connected to a final artifact. |
| Partially Covered | The concept appears in the lesson system and may be practiced, but the full source playbook or implementation depth is not included in Module 1. |
| Referenced | The concept is used as context, guardrail, or supporting logic, but learners are not trained to implement it independently in Module 1. |
| Reserved For Later Module | The concept is important but outside the proper scope of Module 1 and should become a later module or implementation layer. |

## Module 1 Core Concepts

These concepts are core to Module 1 and should remain non-negotiable in review:

- demand generation is not lead generation;
- demand creation, distribution, capture, and buyer enablement;
- three demand states;
- learning intent versus buying intent;
- MOIN;
- industrial buying committee;
- ICP, triggers, fit, and disqualification;
- trusted distribution;
- sales as distribution;
- event and ecosystem signal capture;
- account progression;
- Future Pipeline and Active Focus accounts;
- signal object and routing rules;
- buyer enablement;
- sales-marketing-RevOps cadence;
- dashboard inspection;
- anti-vanity measurement;
- long-cycle industrial adaptation;
- AI-assisted work with human correction;
- 90-day pilot-to-scale logic.

## Important Gaps To Handle Later

These are not failures of Module 1. They are expansion boundaries:

1. Full ABM program design: readiness, goals, 1:1, 1:few, 1:many, expansion, renewal.
2. Full RevOps data architecture: CRM objects, property maps, workflows, dashboards, automation.
3. Platform implementation: HubSpot and Salesforce buildouts.
4. Attribution and pipeline analytics: sourced, influenced, accelerated, expanded, retained, pipeline bridge.
5. Benchmark governance: assumptions, external benchmarks, planning models, replacement with observed data.
6. Partner and distributor operating system: qualification, enablement, governance, channel review.
7. Account research and account POV SOP: research sources, triggers, committee intelligence, activation POV.
8. Discovery and sales execution: discovery discipline, stalled-deal handling, champion coaching.
9. Paid, SEO, and LinkedIn execution: tactical channel modules after the strategic demand system is understood.

## Operator Judgment

Module 1 is strong as an industrial demand generation operating module. It is not a complete conversion of the entire master repo, and it should not pretend to be.

Its proper job is to teach operators how to design, diagnose, and run a 90-day industrial demand generation engine. On that job, the core source intelligence is strongly extracted and operationalized.

The next academy build should use this audit to decide which later modules are needed, instead of overloading Module 1 with every adjacent concept.

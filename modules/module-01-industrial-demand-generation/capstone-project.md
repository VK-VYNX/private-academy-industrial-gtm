# Capstone Project: 90-Day Industrial Demand Generation Pilot

## Assignment

Build a 90-day Industrial Demand Generation Pilot for one selected manufacturing or export category.

The pilot must create trust, account movement, and qualified pipeline by combining buyer education, distribution, signal routing, buyer enablement, and weekly revenue cadence.

## Category Constraints

Choose a category pattern, not a named-company case. Acceptable examples include:

- regulated calibration and validation services;
- pharma or regulated packaging machinery;
- food processing or frying equipment;
- biomass or waste-to-fuel equipment;
- transformers and electrical equipment;
- contract manufacturing or precision fabrication;
- industrial electronics, inspection, or automation-adjacent systems;
- surface engineering or specialty process equipment;
- specialty or process chemicals.

## Required Artifacts

| Artifact | Required Content |
|---|---|
| Executive summary | revenue problem, category focus, pilot scope, decision request |
| ICP definition | sector, account type, triggers, geography, technical fit, commercial fit, exclusions |
| Buying committee map | roles, concerns, proof needs, blockers, coverage plan |
| MOIN grid | buyer questions by role across content, solution, and vendor demand |
| Content plan | assets, demand state, format, SME input, sales use, signal created |
| Distribution plan | channels, trust reason, owner, cadence, follow-up rule |
| Signal-routing rules | signal source, demand state, strength, owner, SLA, next action, false positives |
| Weekly cadence | agenda, board, owners, evidence reviewed, decisions made |
| Success metrics | account movement, committee coverage, signal quality, opportunity quality, pipeline learning |
| Anti-pattern risks | likely failure modes and correction actions |

## 90-Day Pilot Shape

| Phase | Weeks | Operating Focus | Required Output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Phase 1 | 1-2 | scope, ICP, committee, RevOps readiness | pilot charter and account list |
| Phase 2 | 3-4 | MOIN, content audit, proof gaps | MOIN grid and content backlog |
| Phase 3 | 5-8 | content creation, distribution, signal capture | active content and distribution cadence |
| Phase 4 | 9-11 | routing, activation, buyer enablement | Future Pipeline and Active Focus review |
| Phase 5 | 12-13 | learning, decision, scale or repair | executive readout |

## Success Metrics

Use leading indicators before revenue fully materializes:

- target-account reach from priority segments;
- engaged accounts by demand state;
- repeat engagement from target accounts;
- buying committee coverage;
- new Future Pipeline accounts;
- new Active Focus accounts;
- strong signals routed within SLA;
- content assets reused by sales;
- opportunities created with champion, need, timeline, value logic, and next step;
- anti-patterns found and corrected.

Avoid using these as primary success metrics:

- impressions;
- likes;
- raw website traffic;
- generic leads;
- MQL count without fit, role, state, or signal strength;
- unguided content volume.

## Stop, Repair, Scale Criteria

| Decision | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Stop | ICP is weak, signals are low-fit, sales cannot define relevance, or the segment cannot justify effort |
| Repair | content creates engagement but weak movement, distribution is too narrow, committee coverage is missing, or RevOps cannot inspect signals |
| Scale | target accounts move, strong signals route on time, sales uses content, committee proof gaps shrink, and qualified opportunities emerge |

## Submission Standard

The capstone should be field-ready enough that a revenue leader can approve, reject, or repair the pilot without asking what the team actually plans to do.

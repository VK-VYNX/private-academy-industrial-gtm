# Unit 3: ICP, Segments, And Demand Focus

## Concept

Industrial companies often overstate their addressable market. A strong demand generation system starts by narrowing the market to the accounts where the company can create and capture meaningful movement.

An industrial ICP should include:

- sector and application;
- plant, process, project, or compliance trigger;
- geography;
- technical fit;
- commercial fit;
- buying process;
- access path;
- disqualification criteria.

## Industrial Example

A contract manufacturing supplier says it targets "all OEMs." That is too broad for a 90-day pilot.

A sharper ICP:

- export-oriented equipment OEMs;
- recurring tolerance or documentation requirements;
- new product or vendor diversification trigger;
- medium-to-high complexity parts;
- quality team involved in supplier approval;
- procurement open to second-source evaluation;
- exclude one-off low-value fabrication jobs and accounts with no repeat potential.

## Diagnostic Question

Which accounts are valuable enough, specific enough, and reachable enough to justify demand generation effort?

## Group Critique

The group challenges:

- Is the ICP narrow enough for 90 days?
- What should be excluded?
- What signal would prove a segment is moving?
- What would sales need to say "this account is worth action"?

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask the AI agent to score the candidate segments.

Required AI output:

- priority ranking;
- fit rationale;
- demand evidence;
- access path;
- disqualification risks;
- recommended pilot scope.

## Human Correction

Correct the AI for:

- overvaluing market size;
- ignoring buying process complexity;
- missing channel or relationship access;
- recommending accounts sales cannot credibly activate.

## Final Artifact

ICP and demand focus statement:

```text
For the next 90 days, we will focus on [category/account type] in [market/geography] where [trigger] creates a need for [operational outcome]. We will exclude [disqualification rules]. We will measure movement through [account progression signals].
```

# Unit 6: Distribution: The Missing Half

## Concept

Publishing is not distribution.

Industrial demand moves through trusted channels:

- expert and leadership profiles;
- sales sharing;
- associations and export bodies;
- trade shows and technical events;
- distributors and partners;
- consultants and project advisors;
- industry publications;
- website and content hubs;
- search and paid amplification where demand exists.

Choose channels by buyer trust and demand state, not by what is easiest to post.

## Industrial Example

A specialty process equipment supplier publishes useful technical notes, but only on the company blog. The right buyers rarely see them.

The distribution repair:

- convert notes into expert LinkedIn posts;
- give sales a short account-sharing prompt;
- pitch one technical article to an industry publication;
- use a trade association webinar for borrowed trust;
- give distributors a partner-ready proof note;
- retarget engaged target accounts with implementation proof.

## Diagnostic Question

Where does the content need to circulate for the buyer to trust it and for the account team to detect movement?

## Group Critique

Peers challenge:

- Does this rely too heavily on owned publishing?
- Are expert profiles used where trust matters?
- Is sales distributing insight, not just asking for meetings?
- Are events designed before, during, and after the show?
- Are partners and distributors given proof and routing rules?

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask the AI agent to recommend a channel mix by demand state.

Required AI output:

- channel;
- trust reason;
- demand state;
- owner;
- cadence;
- account-level signal;
- routing implication.

## Human Correction

Humans must correct:

- unrealistic channel volume;
- channels without owners;
- paid amplification before content proof;
- trade-show plans without post-event routing;
- distribution that creates no observable account signal.

## Final Artifact

Distribution system with at least five channels and an owner, cadence, signal, and follow-up rule for each.

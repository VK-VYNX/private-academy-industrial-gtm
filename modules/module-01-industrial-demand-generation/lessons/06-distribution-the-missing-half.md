## Concept

Publishing is not distribution.

Industrial demand moves through trust networks:

- expert and leadership profiles;
- sales relationships;
- associations and export bodies;
- exhibitions and technical events;
- distributors and channel partners;
- consultants and project advisors;
- industry publications;
- webinars and roundtables;
- search when buyers already know the category;
- paid amplification when the audience and asset are proven.

Distribution is the operating system that makes content visible, credible, repeated, and trackable.

## Why Distribution Is Different In Industrial Markets

Industrial buyers often trust:

- known experts;
- technical peers;
- industry associations;
- consultants;
- distributors;
- plant visits;
- exhibition conversations;
- trade media;
- supplier engineers;
- customer references;
- field sales people with real application knowledge.

They may not trust:

- generic company-page posts;
- exaggerated product claims;
- unguided paid ads;
- SEO content written for traffic;
- cold outreach with no context;
- AI-written thought leadership with no field insight.

Therefore, industrial distribution must be chosen by buyer trust and decision stage, not by channel convenience.

## Channel Roles

| Channel | Best Role | Demand State | Operator Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Expert LinkedIn profiles | expertise, visibility, relationship warmth | content and solution | use people with credibility, not only company page |
| Sales sharing | account-specific circulation | content, solution, vendor | sales shares insight before asking for meetings |
| Associations and export bodies | borrowed trust and education | content and solution | co-create useful education, not product promotion |
| Trade shows | access, trust, timing, and signal capture | solution and vendor | plan before, during, and after the event |
| Distributors and partners | market sensing and trust transfer | solution and vendor | give proof assets and routing rules |
| Consultants and advisors | specification influence | solution and vendor | educate safe recommendation |
| Industry publications | third-party authority | content and solution | publish technical or market insight |
| Website and content hub | central proof library | all states | organize by buyer question and role |
| Search | late-stage demand capture | solution and vendor | write for specific buyer questions |
| Paid amplification | controlled reach to account clusters | content and solution | amplify proven content, not weak claims |

## Dark Social In Industrial Markets

Industrial dark social is where trust moves without neat attribution.

It can happen in:

- WhatsApp groups;
- exhibition aisles;
- association meetings;
- distributor calls;
- consultant recommendations;
- plant visits;
- private buyer conversations after webinars;
- peer referrals;
- technical forums;
- supplier evaluation committees.

If sales and partners do not capture these signals, the system will undercount the real sources of demand.

Distribution must therefore include a capture rule:

```text
When an account, role, problem, timing, or referral is mentioned in a trusted channel, log it as a signal with source, role, demand state, strength, owner, and next action.
```

## Industrial Example

A composite specialty process equipment supplier has useful technical notes about process repeatability and coating selection. The notes sit on the company blog. Buyers rarely see them before they call for price.

Distribution repair:

| Asset | Distribution Motion | Trust Reason | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| process selection note | SME LinkedIn post series | expert voice is more trusted than company promotion | target account role comments or saves |
| application proof brief | sales shares with Future Pipeline accounts | relevant proof tied to known problem | account replies or forwards internally |
| technical education session | association webinar | borrowed trust from industry body | attendee role and account match |
| partner-ready proof note | distributor enablement | distributors can detect timing | partner flags specific project |
| implementation risk checklist | retarget engaged accounts | repeated visibility to known audience | solution-demand return visit |

The distribution plan creates visibility, trust, and signal. It does not simply publish more.

## Trade Show Operating Logic

Events are not badge collection. They are account progression systems.

| Phase | Required Action | Output |
|---|---|---|
| 8-12 weeks before | define target accounts and meeting thesis | event target list |
| 4-8 weeks before | publish educational content tied to event theme | pre-event demand creation |
| 2-4 weeks before | invite target stakeholders, partners, consultants | meeting pipeline |
| During | log account, role, signal, need, timing, next action | event signal board |
| 24-72 hours after | route accounts by fit and demand state | follow-up ownership |
| 1-3 weeks after | share content hub, consultation, sample, audit, proof | activation or nurture |
| 4-8 weeks after | review progression and opportunities | event ROI by account movement |

## Distributor And Partner Logic

Distributors are not just resellers. They are market sensors and trust carriers.

Partner operating rules:

- define qualified partner signal;
- train partners on ICP and disqualification;
- provide proof assets and buyer questions;
- separate partner-sourced content demand, solution demand, and vendor demand;
- capture source, role, problem, timing, and next action;
- review partner progression monthly;
- protect ownership rules for strategic accounts.

## Diagnostic Question

Where does the content need to circulate for the buyer to trust it and for the account team to detect movement?

Use these prompts:

- Which channels already have buyer trust?
- Which channels can reach the buying committee?
- Which channels create observable account signals?
- Which channels are useful for content demand versus vendor demand?
- Where is sales already in conversation?
- Where do distributors or consultants influence decisions?
- What event or association can create borrowed trust?
- What should be captured after distribution?

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask AI to recommend a channel mix by demand state.

Required AI output:

| Output | Required Standard |
|---|---|
| Channel recommendation | tied to buyer trust and demand state |
| Trust reason | explains why the buyer would believe the channel |
| Owner | names sales, marketing, SME, distributor, partner, or leadership owner |
| Cadence | gives realistic frequency |
| Signal | defines account-level evidence generated |
| Routing implication | explains what happens after signal appears |
| Risk | identifies channel failure mode |

## Human Correction

Humans must repair AI output when it:

- recommends too many channels for the team to operate;
- assumes paid ads can replace trust;
- ignores sales and partner distribution;
- plans events without follow-up routing;
- recommends company-page posting as the main motion;
- gives no owner or cadence;
- creates distribution that cannot generate account evidence.

## RevOps Translation

Distribution must create trackable source evidence.

Useful fields:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Distribution channel | source of exposure or engagement |
| Asset | content or proof asset circulated |
| Account | target account or account cluster |
| Buying role | stakeholder role if known |
| Demand state | content, solution, vendor |
| Signal strength | weak, moderate, strong |
| Source type | owned, social, sales, event, association, partner, distributor, paid, search |
| Follow-up rule | nurture, research, map committee, activate, opportunity review |

## Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Publishing-only | content exists but buyers never see it | build channel plan and owner model |
| Company-page dependency | posts have low reach and trust | move insight through experts and sales |
| Paid-before-proof | ads amplify weak content | validate asset and audience first |
| Event leakage | meetings happen but signals are not routed | create event signal board and 72-hour routing |
| Partner noise | distributors send weak leads | train ICP, proof, and routing rules |
| No dark-social capture | referrals and conversations vanish | sales and partners log source evidence |
| No cadence | distribution happens once | build repeatable circulation plan |

## Completion Standard

This unit is complete only when the learner can explain:

- where buyers already trust information;
- how content will circulate beyond owned publishing;
- how sales will participate;
- how partners, events, or associations will be used;
- what signals each channel creates;
- who owns follow-up.

If the plan says "publish on LinkedIn and blog" without account routing, it fails.

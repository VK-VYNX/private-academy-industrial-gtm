# Unit 8: Measurement, Cadence, And 90-Day Pilot

## Concept

Industrial demand generation becomes real only when the team inspects account movement every week.

The cadence should review:

- target account progression;
- Future Pipeline and Active Focus movement;
- new opportunities;
- stalled accounts;
- content engagement by account;
- buying committee gaps;
- sales conversations and objections;
- proof gaps;
- next plays and owners.

The pilot should be judged by account movement and learning before long-cycle revenue fully materializes.

## Industrial Example

A calibration and validation services provider runs a 90-day pilot for regulated manufacturers. Revenue may not close in 90 days, but the team can still inspect:

- target accounts reached;
- quality and validation roles engaged;
- audit-window signals identified;
- Future Pipeline accounts created;
- Active Focus accounts with named stakeholders;
- technical consultation requests;
- missing compliance proof;
- sales follow-up SLAs met.

## Diagnostic Question

What will the team inspect every week so the pilot improves before the quarter ends?

## Group Critique

Peers must test:

- Are the metrics mostly leading indicators?
- Can RevOps actually capture the evidence?
- Are owners named?
- Are stop, repair, and scale criteria explicit?
- Does the cadence create decisions, or only reporting?

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask the AI agent to assemble the 90-day pilot from prior artifacts.

Required AI output:

- executive summary;
- phase plan;
- weekly cadence;
- success metrics;
- anti-pattern risks;
- stop, repair, scale criteria;
- unresolved assumptions.

## Human Correction

Humans must correct:

- vanity metrics;
- missing owner model;
- scale recommendation without proof;
- weekly meeting that lacks decision rules;
- pilot scope that is too broad for 90 days.

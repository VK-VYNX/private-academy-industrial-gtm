## Concept

Industrial demand generation becomes real only when the team inspects account movement every week.

The weekly cadence is not a reporting meeting. It is an operating rhythm where sales, marketing, RevOps, leadership, and technical owners decide what moved, what stalled, what signal matters, what proof is missing, and what to do next.

In long-cycle industrial markets, revenue may not close inside 90 days. That does not mean the pilot cannot be judged. It means the pilot must measure leading indicators of account movement, committee coverage, signal quality, buyer enablement, and opportunity quality.

## What To Measure

Avoid measuring only volume. Measure movement.

| Weak Metric | Why It Is Insufficient | Stronger Measure |
|---|---|---|
| impressions | can come from weak-fit buyers | target-account reach |
| likes | low buying evidence | engaged accounts by fit and role |
| traffic | may include irrelevant visitors | repeat engagement from ICP accounts |
| MQL count | may ignore fit and demand state | qualified signals by account |
| content volume | does not prove buyer movement | assets that create sales conversations |
| form fills | may include low-fit inquiries | vendor-demand signals with owner and SLA |

Good pilot metrics:

- target-account reach;
- engaged accounts by demand state;
- repeat engagement from target accounts;
- buying committee roles engaged;
- Future Pipeline accounts created;
- Active Focus accounts created;
- strong signals routed within SLA;
- sales conversations created by useful content;
- proof gaps identified and repaired;
- opportunities created with evidence;
- disqualifications that protect effort;
- movement from account progression board to opportunity review.

## Weekly Revenue Cadence

The weekly meeting should inspect evidence and make decisions.

| Agenda Item | Evidence Reviewed | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Progression wins | accounts that moved state | what caused movement and what repeats? |
| Active Focus accounts | strong evidence, current owner, next action | activate, enable, stall, downgrade, or disqualify? |
| Future Pipeline accounts | engagement, trigger, role, research status | research, nurture, map committee, or activate? |
| New signals | source, fit, role, state, strength | route and assign owner |
| Buying committee gaps | missing roles and blockers | build proof, multi-thread, or enable champion |
| Content and proof gaps | objections, sales feedback, weak assets | build, repair, retire, or distribute |
| Stalled accounts | last movement, blocker, missing proof | diagnose and assign repair |
| SLA misses | signals not acted on | fix ownership or process |
| Next commitments | owner and due date | lock next actions |

## 90-Day Pilot Shape

| Phase | Weeks | Operating Focus | Required Output |
|---|---:|---|---|
| 1. Scope and setup | 1-2 | revenue goal, ICP, account list, committee assumptions, RevOps tracking | pilot charter and account progression board |
| 2. MOIN and content backlog | 3-4 | buyer questions, content audit, proof gaps, first assets | MOIN grid and first-five asset plan |
| 3. Distribution and signal capture | 5-8 | publish, circulate, event or partner motion, signal logging | active distribution cadence and signal board |
| 4. Routing and activation | 9-11 | Future Pipeline, Active Focus, committee enablement, sales action | routed signals and buyer enablement assets |
| 5. Readout and decision | 12-13 | movement analysis, learning, risk, scale decision | executive readout and stop, repair, scale recommendation |

The pilot must be scoped narrowly enough that the team can learn. A broad pilot becomes a shallow campaign.

## Industrial Example

A composite calibration and validation services provider runs a 90-day pilot for regulated manufacturers.

Revenue may not close in 90 days, but the team can inspect:

- target accounts reached;
- quality and validation roles engaged;
- audit-window signals identified;
- calibration interval content engagement;
- Future Pipeline accounts created;
- Active Focus accounts with named stakeholders;
- technical consultation requests;
- missing compliance proof;
- sales follow-up SLA completion;
- accounts disqualified because fit or timing is weak.

The pilot is successful if the team proves a repeatable motion:

```text
ICP account with audit or validation trigger sees useful education, engages with role-specific proof, becomes visible in signal routing, gets assigned to an owner, receives the right next action, and moves toward consultation, opportunity review, or qualified future pipeline.
```

## Stop, Repair, Scale Criteria

Define this before launch.

| Decision | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Stop | ICP is weak, signals are low-fit, sales cannot define relevance, value does not justify effort, or distribution cannot reach the buyer |
| Repair | engagement exists but account movement is weak, distribution is narrow, committee coverage is missing, proof gaps block movement, or RevOps cannot inspect signals |
| Scale | target accounts move, strong signals route on time, sales uses content, committee proof gaps shrink, and qualified opportunities emerge |

## Diagnostic Question

What will the team inspect every week so the pilot improves before the quarter ends?

Use these prompts:

- Which accounts moved?
- What evidence caused the movement?
- Which accounts stalled?
- Which buying roles are missing?
- Which content or proof created sales conversations?
- Which signals were false positives?
- Which strong signals missed SLA?
- Which accounts should be disqualified?
- What should be built, routed, or repaired next week?
- What decision does leadership need to make?

## Human Correction

Humans must repair AI output when it:

- uses vanity metrics;
- recommends scale without proof;
- lacks RevOps fields;
- lacks owner model;
- treats the pilot as a campaign;
- ignores sales behavior;
- ignores committee coverage;
- gives a broad plan with no disqualification;
- reports confidently without naming assumptions.

## RevOps Translation

The cadence should operate from a board or dashboard with:

| View | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Account progression board | Cluster ICP, Future Pipeline, Active Focus, Opportunity, Disqualified |
| Signal board | source, state, strength, role, owner, SLA, next action |
| Committee coverage | roles mapped, engaged, enabled, missing, blocker |
| Content and proof gap list | assets to build, repair, or retire |
| Sales action tracker | owner, action, due date, outcome |
| SLA tracker | strong signals acted on within required time |
| Pilot learning log | assumptions tested, findings, next repairs |

## Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Reporting meeting | team reviews numbers without decisions | require decision rule and owner per agenda item |
| Vanity success | pilot celebrates impressions or MQLs | measure account movement and quality |
| No RevOps base | signals cannot be inspected | create signal board before launch |
| Sales disconnected | sales does not use content or signals | define sales role and weekly commitments |
| Scope creep | too many segments and channels | narrow ICP and pilot motion |
| Premature scale | leadership expands before proof | use stop, repair, scale criteria |
| No learning loop | same mistakes repeat weekly | record learning and update ICP, content, distribution, routing |

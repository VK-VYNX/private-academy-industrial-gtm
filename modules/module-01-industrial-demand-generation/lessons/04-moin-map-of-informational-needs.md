# Unit 4: MOIN: Map Of Informational Needs

## Concept

MOIN turns buyer uncertainty into the content roadmap.

Every substantial content asset should clarify:

- Market: which segment or account cluster it is for;
- Objective: what belief, signal, or movement it should create;
- Insight: what non-obvious lesson it teaches;
- Next action: what the buyer or account team should do after engagement.

MOIN is not a topic brainstorm. It is a map of questions that different buying committee roles must answer before an account can move.

## Industrial Example

For an industrial inspection and automation-adjacent supplier, the MOIN is not "write about automation."

Better questions include:

- Quality: why are defects escaping final inspection?
- Plant head: where is throughput being lost?
- Engineering: what data is needed to validate inspection logic?
- Finance: how large is hidden defect cost?
- Procurement: what should be specified to compare vendors safely?

## Diagnostic Question

What does each buyer role need to understand before the account can move to the next demand state?

## Group Critique

The group marks each question:

- green if it reflects real buyer uncertainty;
- yellow if it is useful but too generic;
- red if it is just a product pitch disguised as education.

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask the AI agent to create or repair the MOIN grid.

Required AI output:

- questions by role;
- demand state for each question;
- required asset;
- likely next action;
- source concept used.

## Human Correction

Humans must repair:

- generic questions;
- questions that ignore the buying committee;
- vendor-demand content placed too early;
- missing proof or implementation concerns;
- missing sales use.

## Final Artifact

MOIN grid with at least:

- five committee roles;
- three demand states;
- one required asset per role;
- one sales or RevOps use per asset.

## Integrative Questions

- What content would help the buyer move forward?
- What signal would prove this is working?
- What would sales do with this asset?
- What would an AI agent likely make too generic?

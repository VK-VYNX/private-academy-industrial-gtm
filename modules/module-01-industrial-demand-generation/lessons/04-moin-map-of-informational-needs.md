## Concept

MOIN means Map Of Informational Needs.

It is the discipline of asking: what does each buyer role need to understand before the account can move?

MOIN is not a topic brainstorm. It is not "content pillars." It is not "write about our products." It is a map of buyer uncertainty across demand states and committee roles.

Every substantial content asset should clarify:

- **Market:** which segment or account cluster it is for;
- **Objective:** what belief, signal, or movement it should create;
- **Insight:** what non-obvious lesson it teaches;
- **Next action:** what the buyer or account team should do after engagement.

If an asset cannot answer those four questions, it is probably content volume, not demand generation.

## Why MOIN Matters

Industrial buyers delay decisions when they lack information that reduces risk.

They may need to understand:

- why the problem matters now;
- what the cost of inaction is;
- which solution approaches exist;
- what criteria should be used to compare options;
- what proof is required for technical confidence;
- how implementation will affect operations;
- how finance should think about payback;
- what procurement should specify;
- how the champion should explain the decision internally.

MOIN turns these uncertainties into content and enablement priorities.

## Demand States Inside MOIN

Each buyer question should be placed into a demand state.

| Demand State | Buyer Question Type | Content Role |
|---|---|---|
| Content demand | What is the problem, risk, trend, or operational issue? | educate and build trust |
| Solution demand | What approaches can solve this and how should we compare them? | shape decision criteria |
| Vendor demand | Which supplier is credible, safe, and worth choosing? | prove fit and reduce risk |

The same buyer role can have all three types of questions.

Example for a quality head:

| Demand State | Question |
|---|---|
| Content demand | Why are defects escaping final inspection? |
| Solution demand | What inspection approaches reduce false negatives without slowing throughput? |
| Vendor demand | What proof shows this supplier can validate performance on a similar line? |

## Industrial Example

A composite industrial inspection and automation-adjacent supplier wants to create demand in plants with rising defect cost.

Weak content plan:

- write about automation trends;
- post product demo videos;
- publish brochure excerpts;
- run ads to a contact form.

MOIN-based content plan:

| Buyer Role | Content Demand | Solution Demand | Vendor Demand | Required Asset |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quality head | Why are defects escaping final inspection? | What inspection method reduces false positives and false negatives? | What validation proof exists for similar lines? | false-positive checklist and validation proof note |
| Plant head | Where is throughput being lost? | How can inspection improve without creating a bottleneck? | What is the downtime and service plan? | throughput impact brief |
| Engineering | What data is needed to validate inspection logic? | Which sensor, camera, PLC, and reporting options fit our line? | What integration proof exists? | technical acceptance criteria template |
| Finance | How large is hidden defect cost? | What payback model is credible? | What is the downside risk of implementation? | defect-cost calculator |
| Procurement | What should be specified? | How should suppliers be compared? | What vendor risk should be checked? | specification and vendor comparison checklist |

This is demand generation because it educates the market, equips sales, creates role-specific signals, and reduces internal buying risk.

## MOIN Operating Model

Build MOIN in this order:

1. Choose the ICP segment.
2. List the buying committee roles.
3. For each role, write content-demand questions.
4. For each role, write solution-demand questions.
5. For each role, write vendor-demand questions.
6. Identify the proof asset required.
7. Define how sales would use the asset.
8. Define what signal engagement with the asset creates.
9. Decide whether the asset should be ungated, lightly gated, sales-assisted, or internal enablement.
10. Prioritize the top five assets by account movement potential.

## Diagnostic Question

What does each buyer role need to understand before the account can move to the next demand state?

Use these prompts:

- What does the buyer not yet believe?
- Which role is under-educated?
- Which question blocks internal consensus?
- Which question appears repeatedly in sales calls?
- Which question appears before RFQ?
- Which question appears after technical approval but before purchase?
- What proof would shorten the internal debate?
- What content would make sales more useful?
- What engagement would create a meaningful signal?

## Good Versus Weak MOIN Questions

| Weak Question | Why It Fails | Stronger Question |
|---|---|---|
| What are the benefits of our product? | supplier-centered | What operational risk does the current process create? |
| Why choose us? | vendor demand too early | What criteria should the buyer use to compare approaches? |
| How can we improve efficiency? | too broad | Where is throughput being lost and what process evidence proves it? |
| What is the best machine? | premature vendor selection | What should be specified before comparing machinery suppliers? |
| How much does it cost? | procurement-only | What cost of inaction, downtime, defect, audit, or service risk exists? |

## Group Critique

The group marks each question:

- green if it reflects real buyer uncertainty;
- yellow if it is useful but too generic;
- red if it is a product pitch disguised as education.

For every yellow or red question, the group must ask:

- Which role would ask this?
- At what demand state?
- What decision risk does it reduce?
- What proof would make the answer credible?
- How would sales use the answer?
- What signal would engagement create?

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask AI to create or repair the MOIN grid.

Required AI output:

| Output | Required Standard |
|---|---|
| Buyer role questions | separated by role and demand state |
| Asset recommendation | one asset per critical question or proof gap |
| Sales use | how sales should use each asset without pitching too early |
| RevOps signal | what engagement means and how to route it |
| Assumptions | what the AI is guessing |
| Human questions | what needs sales or SME validation |

## Human Correction

Humans must repair AI output when it:

- creates generic content topics;
- ignores buying committee roles;
- places vendor-demand content too early;
- misses proof, implementation, compliance, procurement, or finance concerns;
- gives no sales use;
- gives no distribution or signal implication;
- uses polished language but no industrial specificity.

## RevOps Translation

MOIN should create trackable signal and content metadata.

Useful fields:

| Field | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Asset demand state | content, solution, vendor |
| Buyer role served | quality, production, maintenance, finance, procurement, leadership, etc. |
| Account cluster | ICP segment or account group |
| Question answered | core buyer uncertainty |
| Sales use case | nurture, committee enablement, objection handling, activation |
| Signal meaning | weak, moderate, or strong evidence |
| Next action | continue education, research, map committee, activate, enable |

## Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Topic brainstorm | list of blog ideas with no buyer role | map role, question, demand state, and proof |
| Product-first content | every asset talks about supplier features | start from buyer uncertainty |
| Missing sales use | marketing creates assets sales ignores | add sales use and conversation context |
| No signal design | engagement does not change account action | define signal strength and routing rule |
| Vendor-demand overload | buyer sees supplier claims before problem belief | build content and solution demand assets first |
| AI generic drift | output sounds plausible but not category-specific | require SME correction and category proof |

## Final Artifact

Submit a MOIN grid with:

- at least five committee roles;
- questions across content, solution, and vendor demand;
- one required asset per critical role;
- sales use for each asset;
- signal created by engagement;
- top five prioritized assets for the 90-day pilot.

## Completion Standard

This unit is complete only when the learner can explain:

- what each buyer role needs to learn;
- why that question matters commercially;
- what content or proof answers it;
- how sales should use it;
- what signal it creates;
- how RevOps should route it.

If the MOIN could be converted into a generic blog calendar without losing meaning, it fails.

## Integrative Questions

- How does this affect sales?

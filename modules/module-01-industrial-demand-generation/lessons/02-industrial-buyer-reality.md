# Unit 2: Industrial Buyer Reality

## Concept

Industrial buyers rarely buy alone. Demand generation must educate a buying committee that includes technical, operational, commercial, quality, procurement, finance, leadership, and sometimes consultant or distributor roles.

Buyer enablement matters because the visible champion often needs to convince people the seller never meets.

## Industrial Example

A regulated packaging machinery supplier has interest from a production head. The production head likes the line-efficiency argument, but the deal stalls because quality needs validation proof, finance needs payback logic, procurement needs vendor comparison, and leadership needs confidence that the project will not disrupt output.

The correction is not more follow-up. The correction is a committee proof system:

- executive case for leadership;
- validation note for quality;
- changeover and downtime plan for production;
- TCO or payback model for finance;
- procurement justification and comparison framework;
- implementation roadmap for operations.

## Diagnostic Question

Who must believe what before this account can move?

## Group Critique

The group reviews whether the map:

- separates roles rather than compressing them into one persona;
- includes blockers, not only supporters;
- names proof that the buyer can circulate internally;
- identifies where sales needs help from marketing or technical experts.

## AI-Assisted Attempt

Ask the AI agent to generate role concerns and proof needs for the selected category.

Required AI output:

- committee roles;
- concern by role;
- required proof by role;
- likely blocker;
- missing content asset.

## Human Correction

Humans must add field reality:

- real sales objections;
- category-specific technical risks;
- buying-process constraints;
- proof that exists versus proof that must be built.

## Final Artifact

Buying committee map with at least five roles and one enablement asset per critical role.

# Worked Example: AI Critique Loop

This example shows how to use AI without accepting generic output.

## Scenario

Category: specialty process chemicals for manufacturers with process stability and documentation requirements.

Task: create three content ideas for demand generation.

## Weak AI Output

| AI Suggestion | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "Top 10 benefits of our chemicals" | product-first and not mapped to buyer questions |
| "Why choose us as your supplier" | vendor demand too early |
| "The future of chemicals in manufacturing" | broad, vague, and not useful to sales |

## Human Correction

The instructor asks:

- Which buyer role has a process stability concern?
- What content-demand question exists before vendor evaluation?
- What signal would this asset create?
- What proof would sales need after engagement?

## Repaired Output

| Asset | Buyer Question | Demand State | Sales Use | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| process instability troubleshooting guide | why are batch results varying? | content | opens diagnostic conversation with production and process roles | target account engages with troubleshooting topic |
| formulation change risk checklist | what should we test before changing inputs? | solution | supports technical consultation and sample planning | checklist use by technical or quality role |
| supplier documentation comparison sheet | what documentation reduces procurement and audit risk? | vendor | helps champion involve procurement and quality | request for documentation review |

## Final Lesson

AI is useful for speed, but humans must enforce:

- industrial specificity;
- role-specific buyer questions;
- demand-state discipline;
- sales and RevOps use;
- proof and signal design;
- confidentiality boundaries.

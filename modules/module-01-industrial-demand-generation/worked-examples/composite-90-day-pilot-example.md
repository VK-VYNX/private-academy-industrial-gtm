# Worked Example: Composite 90-Day Industrial Demand Generation Pilot

This is an anonymized composite example. It is not a named-company case study.

## Category

Industrial inspection and automation-adjacent systems for mid-sized manufacturers with quality escapes, manual inspection bottlenecks, and export documentation pressure.

## Executive Summary

The pilot will focus on manufacturers where defect escape, inspection inconsistency, and quality documentation create operational and commercial risk. The goal is to move qualified target accounts from Cluster ICP to Future Pipeline and Active Focus by educating quality, plant, engineering, finance, and procurement roles before vendor demand appears.

The pilot will not judge success by raw leads. It will judge success by target-account engagement, committee coverage, signal quality, account progression, content reuse by sales, and qualified opportunity creation.

## ICP Definition

| Dimension | Definition |
|---|---|
| Sector | export-oriented discrete manufacturing with visual or dimensional inspection needs |
| Account type | mid-sized plants with quality, production, and engineering leadership |
| Trigger | rising rejection rate, export audit pressure, manual inspection bottleneck, or new production line |
| Technical fit | process has repeatable inspection criteria and measurable defect categories |
| Commercial fit | defect cost or throughput impact can justify diagnostic and solution evaluation |
| Buying process | production, quality, engineering, finance, and procurement involved |
| Access path | expert content, sales relationships, industry events, and quality-focused technical sessions |
| Disqualification | low-value one-off inspection need, no quality owner, no measurable defect cost, no internal owner |

## Buying Committee Map

| Role | Concern | Proof Needed | Asset |
|---|---|---|---|
| Champion | will this solve the defect problem and build credibility internally? | short business case and objection handler | inspection improvement summary |
| Plant head | will throughput or uptime be disrupted? | implementation and downtime plan | phased implementation roadmap |
| Quality | will inspection improve reliability and traceability? | false-positive logic, acceptance criteria, validation data | inspection acceptance criteria template |
| Engineering | will it integrate with existing line logic? | technical dossier and integration assumptions | integration fit note |
| Finance | is the defect cost large enough? | cost-of-quality model and payback logic | defect-cost calculator |
| Procurement | can vendors be compared safely? | specification and vendor-risk framework | inspection supplier comparison checklist |

## MOIN Grid

| Role | Content Demand | Solution Demand | Vendor Demand | Required Asset |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Quality | why are defects escaping final inspection? | what inspection methods reduce false positives? | what validation proof should we ask from suppliers? | false-positive and validation guide |
| Plant head | where is throughput being lost? | how can inspection change without stopping production? | what implementation timeline is realistic? | phased implementation plan |
| Engineering | what data is needed to validate inspection logic? | how should sensors, fixtures, and reporting fit together? | what acceptance criteria define success? | acceptance criteria template |
| Finance | how much do defects really cost? | what savings are realistic? | what payback threshold should approve the project? | defect-cost calculator |
| Procurement | what should be specified? | how should vendors be compared? | what commercial and service risks matter? | supplier comparison checklist |

## Content Plan

| Asset | Demand State | Sales Use | Signal Created |
|---|---|---|---|
| hidden defect-cost explainer | content | opens problem conversation with plant and finance | target-account engagement with cost topic |
| false-positive checklist | content/solution | supports quality and engineering discussion | checklist use by technical role |
| inspection acceptance criteria template | solution | helps sales shape diagnostic or pilot | template download or direct request |
| implementation disruption note | solution/vendor | handles plant and operations risk | repeat engagement from plant or engineering |
| supplier comparison checklist | vendor | supports procurement-safe evaluation | request for vendor comparison or consultation |

## Distribution Plan

| Channel | Role | Cadence | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| expert profile posts | quality and engineering visibility | weekly | engaged named roles from target accounts |
| sales sharing | Future Pipeline accounts | weekly | reply, forward, or meeting request |
| technical webinar | quality and plant committee access | once in pilot | attendee role and questions asked |
| event follow-up | accounts met at trade or association event | 24-72 hours after event | project detail and next action |
| retargeting | engaged target accounts | weeks 6-10 | repeat engagement with solution or vendor asset |
| content hub | all committee roles | always on | multiple roles visiting same account hub |

## Signal Routing

| Signal | Demand State | Strength | Owner | SLA | Next Action |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| single expert post engagement | content | weak | marketing | nurture cycle | continue education |
| repeated engagement by quality role | content/solution | moderate | marketing ops | weekly cadence | add to research queue |
| acceptance criteria template used | solution | moderate | sales and marketing | weekly cadence | map committee and send related proof |
| webinar question about implementation | solution | moderate to strong | sales | 48 hours | create account POV |
| technical consultation request | vendor | strong | sales | same business day | qualify and schedule consultation |
| spec or RFQ shared | vendor | strong | sales and leadership | same business day | opportunity review |

## Weekly Cadence

| Agenda Item | Evidence | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Progression wins | accounts moved to Future Pipeline or Active Focus | repeat the motion that caused movement |
| Active Focus | role coverage, signal strength, next action | activate, enable, or downgrade |
| Future Pipeline | engagement topic and relationship path | research, nurture, or map committee |
| Proof gaps | sales objections and missing assets | assign content or enablement asset |
| Signal SLA | strong signals and response time | repair ownership if missed |
| Metrics | account movement and committee coverage | stop, repair, or scale decisions |

## Anti-Pattern Risks

| Risk | Prevention |
|---|---|
| treating technical content engagement as vendor intent | route by fit, role, demand state, and strength |
| product demos too early | use problem education and acceptance criteria first |
| quality role only, no finance or plant coverage | build defect-cost and implementation assets |
| event conversations lost after the show | route within 72 hours with owner and next action |
| AI-generated generic automation content | require SME input and source-grounded review |

## 90-Day Decision

Scale only if the pilot shows:

- repeat target-account engagement;
- movement into Future Pipeline and Active Focus;
- multiple committee roles engaged;
- strong signals routed within SLA;
- sales uses content in live account conversations;
- qualified opportunities include champion, need, timeline, value logic, and next step.

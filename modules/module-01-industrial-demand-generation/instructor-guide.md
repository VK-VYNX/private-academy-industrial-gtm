# Instructor Guide

## Instructor Role

The instructor is not delivering a passive course. The instructor runs an implementation workshop where learners produce, critique, repair, and operationalize a 90-day industrial demand generation pilot.

The instructor should keep the room focused on three questions:

- What account movement are we trying to create?
- What evidence would prove that movement is real?
- What artifact, owner, field, or cadence will make the movement operational?

## Recommended Format

| Format | Timing | Best For |
|---|---:|---|
| Intensive workshop | 2 full days plus capstone review | agency certification or client kickoff |
| Operating cohort | 8 weekly sessions | client implementation and behavior change |
| AI-agent enablement sprint | 4 half-days | building and QA-ing agent workflows |

## Pre-Work

Ask participants to bring:

- one manufacturing or export category to use throughout the module;
- a target revenue or pipeline problem;
- current ICP or account list, if available;
- examples of existing content, brochures, event materials, or sales decks;
- available CRM fields, dashboard screenshots, or weekly meeting notes;
- known objections from sales, buyers, distributors, or leadership.

## Facilitation Principles

- Force focus before ideation.
- Ask for buyer questions before content topics.
- Ask for signal evidence before sales action.
- Ask for RevOps fields before dashboard opinions.
- Ask for proof needs before collateral requests.
- Ask for disqualification before account expansion.
- Keep AI in the loop, but never let AI be the final judge.

## Critique Standard

Use this critique loop in every unit:

1. The learner presents the artifact in two minutes.
2. Peers identify the strongest operating decision.
3. Peers identify one false assumption, missing proof need, or unclear owner.
4. The AI produces a revised version using the module output contract.
5. Humans reject, repair, or accept the AI changes.
6. The learner updates the final artifact.

## Unit Facilitation Plan

| Unit | Instructor Move | Common Failure To Correct |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Foundations | anchor demand generation in trust, education, distribution, capture, and cadence | learners describe campaigns instead of an operating system |
| 2. Buyer Reality | force committee roles, risks, and proof needs into the discussion | learners reduce the buyer to one persona |
| 3. ICP And Focus | narrow the market with triggers and disqualification | learners overstate addressable market |
| 4. MOIN | turn buyer uncertainty into role-specific questions | learners create topic lists without buyer intent |
| 5. Content Engine | map content to demand state and sales use | learners create assets that sales cannot use |
| 6. Distribution | choose channels by trust and stage | learners publish without a circulation plan |
| 7. Signal Routing | interpret fit, demand state, role, and evidence strength | learners treat all engagement as buying intent |
| 8. Cadence And Pilot | connect artifacts to weekly inspection and 90-day governance | learners define metrics that cannot change behavior |

## AI-Assisted Exercise Rules

Every AI-assisted attempt must include:

- source files used;
- assumptions and unknowns;
- demand state;
- buying committee implications;
- RevOps owner and next action;
- risk or false-positive concern;
- artifact to create or update.

Reject AI outputs that:

- invent named company case studies;
- produce generic SEO or lead-generation advice;
- skip disqualification logic;
- treat impressions, likes, traffic, or generic leads as success;
- write outreach before demand state and account evidence are known;
- recommend scale before pilot proof.

## Capstone Review Meeting

Run the capstone review as an executive pilot inspection, not a class presentation.

Required review sequence:

1. Revenue problem and scope-down logic.
2. ICP, triggers, exclusions, and category fit.
3. Buying committee and proof needs.
4. MOIN and content plan.
5. Distribution and ecosystem plan.
6. Signal-routing rules and RevOps fields.
7. Weekly cadence and owner model.
8. Success metrics and stop, repair, scale criteria.
9. Anti-pattern risk inspection.
10. Decision: approve, repair, or reject.

## Instructor Scoring Guidance

Use [assessment-rubric.md](assessment-rubric.md). A capstone should not pass if it lacks any of these:

- narrow ICP and disqualification;
- buying committee with role-specific proof;
- demand-state routing;
- distribution beyond publishing;
- signal SLA and owner;
- weekly cadence;
- anti-pattern risk review;
- executive summary that a leadership sponsor could understand.

## Executive Summary

The pilot will focus on manufacturers where defect escape, manual inspection bottlenecks, and documentation pressure create measurable quality and throughput risk.

The demand thesis is that these buyers need education before vendor evaluation. Quality, production, engineering, finance, and procurement roles need different proof before the account can move. The 90-day pilot will create useful content, distribute it through trusted channels, route signals by demand state, and inspect account movement weekly.

## ICP Definition

| Dimension | Answer |
|---|---|
| Priority sector | export-oriented discrete manufacturing with inspection and documentation pressure |
| Account type | mid-sized plants with quality, production, and engineering ownership |
| Trigger | rising rejection rate, audit pressure, manual inspection bottleneck, or new line |
| Technical fit | repeatable inspection criteria and measurable defect categories |
| Commercial fit | defect cost or throughput impact can justify evaluation |
| Buying process | quality, plant, engineering, finance, procurement |
| Access path | expert content, sales relationships, technical sessions, events |
| Disqualification | no quality owner, no measurable defect cost, one-off low-value need |

## Buying Committee Map

| Role | Concern | Proof Needed | Missing Asset |
|---|---|---|---|
| Champion | will this solve the problem and build internal credibility? | short business case and objection handler | inspection improvement summary |
| Plant head | will implementation disrupt production? | downtime and rollout plan | phased implementation roadmap |
| Quality | will inspection improve reliability and traceability? | false-positive logic and validation criteria | validation guide |
| Engineering | will it integrate with the line? | technical assumptions and acceptance criteria | integration fit note |
| Finance | is defect cost large enough? | cost model and payback logic | defect-cost calculator |
| Procurement | can suppliers be compared safely? | vendor-risk and specification framework | supplier comparison checklist |

## Weekly Cadence

| Agenda Item | Evidence | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| progression wins | accounts moved to Future Pipeline or Active Focus | repeat the motion |
| Future Pipeline | engagement topic and relationship path | research, nurture, or map committee |
| Active Focus | role coverage and next step | activate, enable, downgrade, or disqualify |
| proof gaps | sales objections | assign asset owner |
| signal SLA | strong signals and response time | repair ownership |

## Success Metrics

- engaged target accounts by demand state;
- repeat engagement from target accounts;
- buying committee roles identified;
- Future Pipeline accounts created;
- Active Focus accounts created;
- strong signals routed within SLA;
- sales reuse of content assets;
- opportunities with champion, need, timeline, value logic, and next step.

## Anti-Pattern Risks

| Risk | Prevention |
|---|---|
| technical engagement treated as vendor intent | route by fit, role, state, and strength |
| product demo too early | use problem education and acceptance criteria first |
| quality-only engagement | create finance and plant proof assets |
| event conversations decay | route within 72 hours |
| AI content becomes generic | require SME review and quality gates |

## Decision

Approve with weekly inspection. The pilot is narrow, signal-aware, and operational enough to launch.

# Quality Gates

## Purpose

Use these gates before delivering Module 1 artifacts to a client, leadership sponsor, sales team, RevOps team, or AI agent.

## Gate 1: Confidentiality

Pass standard:

- examples are anonymized composites;
- no restricted company names appear;
- no private client facts appear;
- no invented named case studies appear.

Reject if:

- the artifact implies a real company case study without approval;
- the artifact uses confidential inspiration as evidence;
- the AI invents a named customer, supplier, or competitor story.

## Gate 2: Industrial Specificity

Pass standard:

- names a sector or category;
- includes a trigger or operational problem;
- includes buying roles beyond "decision maker";
- includes proof needs;
- includes disqualification logic.

Reject if:

- the plan could apply to any generic B2B company;
- the content could be used unchanged for SaaS;
- the ICP is only broad industry language.

## Gate 3: Demand-State Discipline

Pass standard:

- content demand, solution demand, and vendor demand are separated;
- weak learning signals are not treated as buying intent;
- strong vendor-demand signals are routed quickly.

Reject if:

- every engagement becomes a sales task;
- every asset is vendor pitch material;
- demand state is absent from signal routing.

## Gate 4: Sales Use

Pass standard:

- every major content asset has a sales use;
- sales knows when to share, when to wait, and when to activate;
- buyer enablement exists for committee gaps.

Reject if:

- sales is asked only to "follow up on leads";
- content has no role in account progression;
- assets are not usable in real conversations.

## Gate 5: RevOps Actionability

Pass standard:

- signals have owner, SLA, source, demand state, strength, and next action;
- account progression states are visible;
- weekly cadence has evidence and decision rules.

Reject if:

- dashboards only show activity volume;
- no one owns signals;
- the team cannot inspect movement weekly.

## Gate 6: Measurement Quality

Pass standard:

- primary measures are account movement, committee coverage, signal quality, sales use, opportunity quality, and learning;
- vanity metrics are secondary context only.

Reject if primary success is:

- impressions;
- likes;
- raw traffic;
- generic leads;
- unguided MQL count;
- content volume.

## Gate 7: AI Governance

Pass standard:

- AI output lists assumptions and unknowns;
- human correction is documented;
- source concepts are visible;
- output has risks and false-positive concerns.

Reject if:

- AI output is accepted without review;
- AI writes generic advice;
- AI invents evidence;
- AI recommends scale without pilot proof.

## Minimum Passing Standard

A Module 1 capstone cannot pass if it lacks any of these:

- ICP definition;
- disqualification rules;
- buying committee map;
- MOIN grid;
- signal-routing rules;
- weekly cadence;
- success metrics based on account movement;
- anti-pattern risk register.

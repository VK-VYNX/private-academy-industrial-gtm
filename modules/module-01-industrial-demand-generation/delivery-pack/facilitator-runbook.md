# Facilitator Runbook

## Facilitator Mission

Your job is not to teach a course. Your job is to move learners from vague demand generation language to field-ready operating artifacts.

The room should leave with:

- a narrower market focus;
- a clearer buying committee;
- a MOIN grid;
- a content and distribution plan;
- signal-routing rules;
- a weekly cadence;
- a 90-day pilot that leadership can approve, repair, or reject.

## Facilitation Posture

Be strict about operational clarity.

When learners say "awareness," ask what account movement proves it.

When learners say "content," ask which buyer question it answers.

When learners say "lead," ask for ICP fit, role, demand state, signal strength, owner, SLA, and next action.

When learners use AI, ask what humans corrected.

## Room Setup

Required:

- selected category for each learner or team;
- printed or shared workbook;
- [flowcharts/](flowcharts/) opened for visual orientation;
- access to AI prompt library;
- whiteboard or shared canvas;
- sample current content or sales material;
- one facilitator score sheet per team.

Recommended roles per team:

- sales voice;
- marketing voice;
- RevOps voice;
- technical or SME voice;
- executive sponsor voice.

## Unit Facilitation Scripts

### Unit 1: Foundations

Visual: [flowcharts/01-industrial-demand-generation-operating-system.md](flowcharts/01-industrial-demand-generation-operating-system.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
We are not here to collect more leads. We are here to design an operating system that makes the right industrial accounts more educated, more trusting, more visible, and more ready for qualified sales action.
```

Push learners to identify the weakest system layer.

Critique questions:

- What account movement is missing?
- Which activity is being overvalued?
- What signal should RevOps capture?

### Unit 2: Buyer Reality

Visual: [flowcharts/03-industrial-buyer-committee-map.md](flowcharts/03-industrial-buyer-committee-map.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
The buyer is not one person. The buyer is a committee with different fears, proof needs, and blockers.
```

Push learners to name at least five roles.

Critique questions:

- Which role can block the deal after technical approval?
- What proof does the champion need to circulate internally?
- Which role has no content coverage today?

### Unit 3: ICP And Focus

Visual: [flowcharts/04-icp-focus-and-disqualification.md](flowcharts/04-icp-focus-and-disqualification.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
Broad markets create weak demand systems. A 90-day pilot needs a sharp target and explicit exclusions.
```

Push learners to remove weak-fit accounts.

Critique questions:

- Which segment is reachable in 90 days?
- What trigger creates urgency?
- What should sales stop chasing?

### Unit 4: MOIN

Visual: [flowcharts/05-moin-to-content-roadmap.md](flowcharts/05-moin-to-content-roadmap.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
MOIN is not a list of topics. It is the buyer's uncertainty translated into required assets.
```

Push learners to write questions, not titles.

Critique questions:

- Is this a real buyer question?
- Which demand state does it belong to?
- What asset would answer it?

### Unit 5: Content Engine

Visual: [flowcharts/06-content-engine-to-signal.md](flowcharts/06-content-engine-to-signal.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
Content earns its place only if it helps the buyer move and helps sales act with relevance.
```

Push learners to define SME input and sales use.

Critique questions:

- Is this asset useful before a sales meeting?
- What signal does it create?
- What proof is missing?

### Unit 6: Distribution

Visual: [flowcharts/07-distribution-ecosystem.md](flowcharts/07-distribution-ecosystem.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
Publishing is not distribution. Industrial trust travels through people, events, associations, partners, distributors, and repeated useful visibility.
```

Push learners to go beyond owned channels.

Critique questions:

- Who will carry this insight?
- Why will the buyer trust this channel?
- What signal can we capture?

### Unit 7: Signal Routing

Visual: [flowcharts/08-signal-routing-and-account-progression.md](flowcharts/08-signal-routing-and-account-progression.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
Every signal must pass through fit, demand state, role, and evidence strength before sales action.
```

Push learners to slow down weak signals and speed up strong vendor-demand signals.

Critique questions:

- What is the false-positive risk?
- Who owns the next action?
- What is the SLA?

### Unit 8: Cadence And Pilot

Visual: [flowcharts/10-weekly-revenue-cadence.md](flowcharts/10-weekly-revenue-cadence.md)

Opening prompt:

```text
The weekly cadence is where the strategy becomes real. If it is not inspected, it is not operated.
```

Push learners to define stop, repair, scale criteria.

Critique questions:

- What will we inspect weekly?
- What decision does each metric inform?
- What would justify scale?

## Critique Loop

Use this loop for every unit:

1. Learner presents the artifact in two minutes.
2. Peers identify one strong operating decision.
3. Peers identify one weak assumption.
4. AI produces a revision or critique.
5. Humans accept, repair, or reject AI suggestions.
6. Learner updates the artifact.
7. Facilitator checks against quality gates.

## Timeboxing

| Activity | Default Time |
|---|---:|
| concept framing | 10 minutes |
| individual work | 20 minutes |
| AI-assisted attempt | 10 minutes |
| group critique | 20 minutes |
| human correction | 15 minutes |
| artifact lock | 5 minutes |

## Red Flag Interventions

| Red Flag | Facilitator Response |
|---|---|
| broad ICP | ask for trigger, access path, value threshold, and exclusions |
| generic content topic | ask for buyer role, question, demand state, and proof |
| sales follow-up on weak signal | ask for fit, role, strength, and false-positive risk |
| vanity metric | ask what account movement the metric changes |
| AI output accepted as final | ask what humans corrected |
| channel list without owner | ask who will do it next week |
| no RevOps field | ask how the weekly cadence will inspect it |

## Capstone Review

Run capstone review like an executive pilot inspection.

Required decision:

- approve for implementation;
- repair before implementation;
- reject and restart the scope.

Do not pass a capstone that lacks signal routing, weekly cadence, or disqualification logic.

## Instructor Use

Use these notes as the instructor depth layer. The projected slides are intentionally concise so the instructor can teach, diagnose, and run artifact production without turning the deck into a handout.

## Slide 01: Industrial Demand Generation Foundations

- Open by naming this as 20 min of operating work, not a lecture.
- Anchor the room on the lesson output: Demand system diagnostic with three priority repairs.
- Remind participants to keep one manufacturing or export category consistent through the workshop.

## Slide 02: Lesson Promise

- Diagnose whether the company has a demand generation engine or only scattered marketing activity, lead capture, and sales heroics.
- The required output is: One focus repair, one content or distribution repair, and one signal, owner, or cadence repair.
- Do not move on until learners know what they will submit at the end of the lesson.

## Slide 03: Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

- Industrial demand often exists before it is visible in a form, RFQ, or sales call.
- Buyers learn through consultants, distributors, exhibitions, peers, technical experts, and internal committees.
- Lead volume can rise while high-fit account movement stays flat.
- The operating system must connect trust, education, distribution, signal interpretation, buyer enablement, and weekly cadence.
- Instructor move: ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

## Slide 04: Core Framework

- The demand engine only works when all seven jobs connect.
- Revenue goal and market focus
- ICP and buying committee
- MOIN and proof assets
- Trusted distribution
- Account-level signals
- Signal routing and buyer enablement
- Weekly learning loop
- Visual companion: 01-industrial-demand-generation-operating-system.svg.

## Slide 05: Mental Model

- Separate the four motions before diagnosing the system.
- Creation: Teach the market why the problem or opportunity matters.
- Distribution: Move insight through channels the buyer already trusts.
- Capture: Interpret account-level evidence by fit, role, state, and strength.
- Enablement: Help champions move the committee when the supplier is not in the room.
- Ask the room which part would break first in their current operating system.

## Slide 06: Anonymized Industrial Example

- Composite example: Composite food processing equipment supplier.
- The team attends exhibitions, collects visiting cards, posts product images, and celebrates inquiry volume.
- Sales complains that most RFQs are low quality and price-led.
- The repair is to focus on export-oriented snack manufacturers with throughput, oil efficiency, hygiene, or export-quality triggers.
- The operating measure becomes target account progression, not inquiry count alone.
- Keep the example anonymized and category-level. Do not invite named-company comparisons.

## Slide 07: Diagnostic Questions

- Where is the current system confusing activity with account movement?
- Are we measuring leads or movement of high-fit accounts?
- Do we know which buying committee roles are engaging?
- Can sales explain why an account should be acted on now?
- Do we have content for content, solution, and vendor demand?
- Does RevOps capture source, state, strength, owner, SLA, and next action?
- Use the prompts as a rapid room diagnostic before the artifact exercise.

## Slide 08: Exercise Setup

- Demand system diagnostic: 6 min individual, 4 min pair review.
- Choose one manufacturing or export category.
- Score the nine system layers: market focus, ICP, demand creation, distribution, intelligence, activation, enablement, cadence.
- Name one repair in focus, one in content or distribution, and one in signal, owner, or cadence.
- Expected artifact: Demand system diagnostic with three priority repairs.

## Slide 09: Group Critique

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, then the group repairs the artifact.
- Is the weakness a focus, content, distribution, routing, enablement, or cadence problem?
- What is the commercial consequence?
- Which buying role is affected?
- What would sales need before acting?
- What would RevOps need to capture?

## Slide 10: Structured Drafting Drill

- Use this as the structured drafting brief.
- Using the selected category, current assets, current channels, sales feedback, and CRM fields, classify the current demand motion as demand creation, demand capture, lead collection, or mixed. Identify the weakest operating layer, one practical repair that can start within two weeks, one account-level signal to track, and the validation questions that require sales, RevOps, SME, or leadership input.
- Require the draft to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.
- Names the missing layer
- Explains the anti-pattern and commercial risk
- Defines signal, owner, and cadence
- Lists field-validation questions

## Slide 11: Human Quality Control

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Reject more-content recommendations with no distribution path.
- Reject lead magnets before buyer trust exists.
- Reject impressions, traffic, likes, and MQLs as primary success.
- Reject activation before state and signal strength are known.
- Reject invented named-company examples.
- Instructor move: ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality is missing.

## Slide 12: RevOps Translation

- The diagnostic must become inspectable fields.
- Account fit
- Demand state
- Signal source and strength
- Buying role
- Progression state
- Owner, SLA, and next action
- If this cannot be represented in a CRM field, board, owner model, or temporary working table, it is not operational yet.

## Slide 13: Failure Modes / Anti-Patterns

- Use these anti-patterns to normalize rejection of weak work.
- Lead-gen disguise: Forms and MQLs dominate -> Reset around account movement
- Campaign calendar thinking: Content planned by month -> Rebuild around MOIN and sales use
- Owned-channel dependency: Publishing without circulation -> Add expert, sales, partner, event, and association paths
- RevOps invisibility: No signal object or progression state -> Define fields and weekly cadence board

## Slide 14: Final Artifact

- Learners submit: Demand system diagnostic with three priority repairs.
- One category or segment focus
- Current-state diagnosis across operating layers
- Three priority repairs
- One account movement signal
- One RevOps tracking requirement
- One sales behavior change
- One anti-pattern risk
- Exit gate: the artifact must change sales behavior, RevOps tracking, content decisions, or weekly cadence.

## Slide 15: Transition

- Once the room agrees that movement matters more than activity, the next question is who inside the buying organization must move.
- Next: Lesson 02 - Industrial Buyer Reality.
- Ask learners what artifact they are carrying forward before opening the next deck.

## Instructor Use

Use these notes as the instructor depth layer. The projected slides are intentionally concise so the instructor can teach, diagnose, and run artifact production without turning the deck into a handout.

## Slide 01: Content Engine For Industrial Demand

- Open by naming this as 22 min of operating work, not a lecture.
- Anchor the room on the lesson output: Content plan by demand state.
- Remind participants to keep one manufacturing or export category consistent through the workshop.

## Slide 02: Lesson Promise

- Turn MOIN into an industrial content engine that creates trust, supports sales, produces account signals, and reduces buying committee risk.
- The required output is: A content plan by demand state with SME input, sales use, distribution path, and signal design for each asset.
- Do not move on until learners know what they will submit at the end of the lesson.

## Slide 03: Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

- Industrial content is not a publishing calendar.
- Useful assets help buyers understand problems, compare approaches, reduce risk, and explain the case internally.
- The same assets help sales warm accounts, interpret demand state, support champions, and identify proof gaps.
- Content without sales use, distribution, or signal design becomes volume.
- Instructor move: ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

## Slide 04: Core Framework

- Every asset needs a job.
- Problem education
- Application education
- Decision criteria
- Proof
- Buyer enablement
- Demand capture
- Sales support
- Visual companion: 06-content-engine-to-signal.svg.

## Slide 05: Mental Model

- Build by demand state, not by calendar slot.
- Content demand: Risk guide, trend note, benchmark, process explainer.
- Solution demand: Selection guide, calculator, checklist, application framework.
- Vendor demand: Technical dossier, validation proof, TCO, implementation plan.
- Buyer enablement: Internal case, objection handler, procurement or finance proof.
- Ask the room which part would break first in their current operating system.

## Slide 06: Anonymized Industrial Example

- Composite example: Composite transformer and electrical equipment supplier.
- The current asset base is brochures, certificates, and tender responses.
- Sales says buyers compare price late and consultants often control specifications before the supplier enters.
- The upstream content engine covers lifecycle losses, specification clarity, service readiness, vendor comparison, and TCO.
- Each asset has a buyer role, demand state, sales use, and signal.
- Keep the example anonymized and category-level. Do not invite named-company comparisons.

## Slide 07: Diagnostic Questions

- Which content assets would make the buyer smarter, safer, and more able to move internally?
- What buyer question appears before the RFQ?
- What objection appears repeatedly in sales calls?
- What proof does the champion need internally?
- Which committee role is under-served?
- Which asset requires SME insight before AI can draft it?
- Which existing asset should be repaired instead of replaced?
- Use the prompts as a rapid room diagnostic before the artifact exercise.

## Slide 08: Exercise Setup

- Build the first-five content plan: 8 min asset plan, 4 min priority call.
- Choose at least two content-demand assets, two solution-demand assets, and one vendor-demand or buyer-enablement asset.
- For each asset, define buyer question, SME input, sales use, distribution path, signal created, and quality risk.
- Label build now, repair, or later.
- Expected artifact: Content plan by demand state.

## Slide 09: Group Critique

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, AI revises, humans accept or repair.
- Is the asset useful before the buyer is ready to talk to sales?
- Does it include field insight, technical proof, or SME judgment?
- Can sales use it without rewriting?
- Does it reduce a specific decision risk?
- Can it be distributed through a trusted channel?
- Would a serious industrial buyer respect it?

## Slide 10: AI-Assisted Exercise Prompt

- Use this as the AI prompt skeleton.
- Turn the MOIN grid into a content plan. For each asset, name the buyer question, demand state, format, SME input, sales use, distribution path, signal design, and quality risk. Flag where human technical or field validation is required before drafting.
- Require the AI to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.
- Assets tied to role, question, and demand state
- Specific SME input required
- Sales use and account context
- Signal design and routing implication
- Distribution path and quality risk

## Slide 11: Human Correction / Quality Control

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Remove generic blog ideas.
- Remove product brochures disguised as education.
- Reject claims unsupported by proof.
- Reject assets without a distribution path.
- Reject assets sales cannot use.
- Reject AI-generated technical claims without SME validation.
- Instructor move: ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality the AI could not know.

## Slide 12: RevOps Translation

- Content must connect to account movement.
- Asset ID
- Demand state
- Buyer role
- Account cluster
- Signal strength
- Sales use
- Next action
- Proof gap
- If this cannot be represented in a CRM field, board, owner model, or temporary working table, it is not operational yet.

## Slide 13: Failure Modes / Anti-Patterns

- Use these anti-patterns to normalize rejection of weak work.
- Content volume strategy: Many posts, little movement -> Prioritize by buyer uncertainty and signal value
- Product-first content: Features before problem belief -> Build problem and solution education first
- AI filler: Polished generic language -> Require SME input and proof
- No distribution path: Content sits on website -> Assign channel, owner, cadence, and sales motion

## Slide 14: Final Artifact

- Learners submit: Content plan by demand state.
- Two content-demand assets
- Two solution-demand assets
- One vendor-demand or buyer-enablement asset
- SME input requirement per asset
- Sales use per asset
- Distribution path per asset
- Signal design per asset
- Exit gate: the artifact must change sales behavior, RevOps tracking, content decisions, or weekly cadence.

## Slide 15: Transition

- A good asset still fails if the right buyers never see it. The next lesson is the missing half: trusted distribution.
- Next: Lesson 06 - Distribution: The Missing Half.
- Ask learners what artifact they are carrying forward before opening the next deck.

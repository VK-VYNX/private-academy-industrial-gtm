## Instructor Use

Use these notes as the instructor depth layer. The projected slides are intentionally concise so the instructor can teach, diagnose, and run artifact production without turning the deck into a handout.

## Slide 01: MOIN: Map Of Informational Needs

- Open by naming this as 22 min of operating work, not a lecture.
- Anchor the room on the lesson output: MOIN grid.
- Remind participants to keep one manufacturing or export category consistent through the workshop.

## Slide 02: Lesson Promise

- Convert buyer uncertainty into a role-specific content and proof roadmap.
- The required output is: A MOIN grid that maps questions by role and demand state, then translates them into content, sales use, signal design, and proof gaps.
- Do not move on until learners know what they will submit at the end of the lesson.

## Slide 03: Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

- Industrial buyers delay decisions when information does not reduce risk.
- Content topics are weak unless they map to buyer uncertainty, role, demand state, and proof need.
- MOIN prevents product-first content and premature vendor claims.
- The map tells marketing what to build, sales how to use it, and RevOps what engagement means.
- Instructor move: ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

## Slide 04: Core Framework

- MOIN maps buyer uncertainty by role and demand state.
- Choose the ICP segment
- List committee roles
- Write content-demand questions
- Write solution-demand questions
- Write vendor-demand questions
- Identify proof assets
- Define sales use and signal meaning
- Visual companion: 05-moin-to-content-roadmap.svg.

## Slide 05: Mental Model

- Every substantial asset must answer four operating questions.
- Market: Which segment or account cluster is this for?
- Objective: What belief, signal, or movement should it create?
- Insight: What non-obvious lesson does it teach?
- Next action: What should the buyer or account team do after engagement?
- Ask the room which part would break first in their current operating system.

## Slide 06: Anonymized Industrial Example

- Composite example: Composite inspection and automation-adjacent supplier.
- The weak plan is automation trends, product demos, brochure excerpts, and ads to a form.
- The MOIN plan starts with defect cost, inspection method comparison, validation proof, integration criteria, TCO, and procurement specification.
- Questions are separated for quality, plant, engineering, finance, and procurement roles.
- The result is demand generation because it educates, equips sales, creates signals, and reduces internal risk.
- Keep the example anonymized and category-level. Do not invite named-company comparisons.

## Slide 07: Diagnostic Questions

- What does each buyer role need to understand before the account can move to the next demand state?
- What does the buyer not yet believe?
- Which role is under-educated?
- Which question blocks internal consensus?
- Which question appears repeatedly in sales calls?
- What proof would shorten the internal debate?
- What engagement would create a meaningful signal?
- Use the prompts as a rapid room diagnostic before the artifact exercise.

## Slide 08: Exercise Setup

- Build the MOIN grid: 8 min grid, 4 min prioritization.
- Pick five committee roles.
- Write content, solution, and vendor-demand questions for each.
- Name the required asset, sales use, and signal created.
- Prioritize the top five assets by account movement potential.
- Expected artifact: MOIN grid.

## Slide 09: Group Critique

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, then the group repairs the artifact.
- Mark each question green, yellow, or red.
- Ask which role would ask it.
- Ask which demand state it belongs to.
- Ask which decision risk it reduces.
- Ask what proof makes the answer credible.
- Ask how sales would use the answer.

## Slide 10: Structured Drafting Drill

- Use this as the structured drafting brief.
- Draft or repair a MOIN grid for the selected ICP. Separate buyer questions by role and demand state. Recommend assets, sales use, RevOps signal meaning, assumptions, and field-validation questions. Avoid generic content topics and vendor-demand claims before buyer belief exists.
- Require the draft to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.
- Questions separated by role and demand state
- One asset per critical question or proof gap
- Sales use without pitching too early
- Signal meaning and routing implication
- Assumptions and validation questions

## Slide 11: Human Quality Control

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Reject generic topics and content pillars.
- Reject output that ignores committee roles.
- Reject vendor-demand assets placed too early.
- Reject missing compliance, procurement, implementation, or finance concerns.
- Reject polished language with no industrial specificity.
- Instructor move: ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality is missing.

## Slide 12: RevOps Translation

- MOIN creates content metadata and signal meaning.
- Asset demand state
- Buyer role served
- Account cluster
- Question answered
- Sales use case
- Signal meaning
- Next action
- If this cannot be represented in a CRM field, board, owner model, or temporary working table, it is not operational yet.

## Slide 13: Failure Modes / Anti-Patterns

- Use these anti-patterns to normalize rejection of weak work.
- Topic brainstorm: Blog ideas with no role -> Map role, question, demand state, and proof
- Product-first content: Assets start with supplier features -> Start from buyer uncertainty
- Missing sales use: Assets are ignored by sales -> Add sales context and use case
- No signal design: Engagement changes no action -> Define signal strength and routing rule

## Slide 14: Final Artifact

- Learners submit: MOIN grid.
- At least five committee roles
- Questions across content, solution, and vendor demand
- One required asset per critical role
- Sales use for each asset
- Signal created by engagement
- Top five prioritized assets for the pilot
- Exit gate: the artifact must change sales behavior, RevOps tracking, content decisions, or weekly cadence.

## Slide 15: Transition

- MOIN tells us what buyers need to learn. The next lesson turns those questions into a content engine sales will actually use.

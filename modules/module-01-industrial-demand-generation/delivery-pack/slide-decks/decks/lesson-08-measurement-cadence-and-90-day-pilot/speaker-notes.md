## Instructor Use

Use these notes as the instructor depth layer. The projected slides are intentionally concise so the instructor can teach, diagnose, and run artifact production without turning the deck into a handout.

## Slide 01: Measurement, Cadence, And 90-Day Pilot

- Open by naming this as 26 min of operating work, not a lecture.
- Anchor the room on the lesson output: Weekly cadence and 90-day pilot plan.
- Remind participants to keep one manufacturing or export category consistent through the workshop.

## Slide 02: Lesson Promise

- Assemble prior artifacts into a 90-day Industrial Demand Generation Pilot and define the weekly revenue cadence that keeps sales, marketing, RevOps, leadership, and technical teams aligned.
- The required output is: A weekly cadence model and pilot plan with success metrics, owners, evidence, and stop, repair, scale criteria.
- Do not move on until learners know what they will submit at the end of the lesson.

## Slide 03: Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

- Industrial demand generation becomes real only when the team inspects account movement every week.
- Revenue may not close inside 90 days, but the pilot can still prove movement, committee coverage, signal quality, and opportunity quality.
- Cadence is not reporting. It is a decision rhythm.
- The pilot must be narrow enough to learn before leadership decides to stop, repair, or scale.
- Instructor move: ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

## Slide 04: Core Framework

- Measure movement, not volume.
- Target-account reach
- Engaged accounts by demand state
- Repeat engagement from ICP accounts
- Buying committee roles engaged
- Future Pipeline and Active Focus accounts
- Strong signals routed within SLA
- Sales conversations created by useful content
- Proof gaps found and repaired
- Visual companion: 10-weekly-revenue-cadence.svg.

## Slide 05: Mental Model

- The weekly meeting makes decisions from evidence.
- Progression: Which accounts moved state, and why?
- Routing: Which new signals need owner, SLA, or downgrade?
- Proof gaps: Which role or objection is blocking movement?
- Next commitments: What will be built, routed, repaired, or disqualified this week?
- Ask the room which part would break first in their current operating system.

## Slide 06: Anonymized Industrial Example

- Composite example: Composite calibration and validation services provider.
- The pilot targets regulated manufacturers with audit or validation triggers.
- Revenue may not close in 90 days, so the team inspects reach, quality and validation roles engaged, audit-window signals, Future Pipeline accounts, Active Focus accounts, consultation requests, proof gaps, SLA completion, and disqualifications.
- The pilot succeeds if a repeatable motion is visible from education to signal routing to owner action to consultation, opportunity review, or qualified future pipeline.
- Keep the example anonymized and category-level. Do not invite named-company comparisons.

## Slide 07: Diagnostic Questions

- What will the team inspect every week so the pilot improves before the quarter ends?
- Which accounts moved?
- What evidence caused movement?
- Which accounts stalled?
- Which buying roles are missing?
- Which signals were false positives?
- Which strong signals missed SLA?
- What decision does leadership need to make?
- Use the prompts as a rapid room diagnostic before the artifact exercise.

## Slide 08: Exercise Setup

- Build the cadence and pilot outline: 8 min cadence board, 6 min pilot phases.
- Build the weekly cadence agenda with evidence reviewed, owner, decision rule, and output.
- Draft the five pilot phases: scope, MOIN/content, distribution/signals, routing/activation, readout/decision.
- Define stop, repair, and scale criteria before launch.
- Expected artifact: Weekly cadence and 90-day pilot plan.

## Slide 09: Group Critique

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, AI revises, humans accept or repair.
- Are the metrics mostly leading indicators?
- Can RevOps capture the evidence?
- Are owners named?
- Are stop, repair, and scale criteria explicit?
- Does the cadence create decisions or only reporting?
- Does the pilot connect content, distribution, routing, sales action, and buyer enablement?

## Slide 10: AI-Assisted Exercise Prompt

- Use this as the AI prompt skeleton.
- Assemble the 90-day pilot from prior artifacts. Include executive summary, phase plan, weekly cadence, success metrics, anti-pattern risks, stop-repair-scale criteria, and unresolved assumptions requiring human validation. Keep metrics focused on account movement and quality, not vanity activity.
- Require the AI to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.
- Clear revenue problem, focus, pilot logic, and decision request
- 90-day sequence with owners and outputs
- Cadence with evidence and decisions
- Success metrics for movement, coverage, signal quality, sales use, and opportunity quality
- Stop, repair, scale criteria tied to evidence

## Slide 11: Human Correction / Quality Control

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Reject vanity metrics.
- Reject scale recommendations without proof.
- Reject plans with no RevOps fields or owner model.
- Reject campaign framing.
- Reject broad plans with no disqualification.
- Reject confident summaries that hide assumptions.
- Instructor move: ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality the AI could not know.

## Slide 12: RevOps Translation

- The cadence runs from boards, not memory.
- Account progression board
- Signal board
- Committee coverage
- Content and proof gap list
- Sales action tracker
- SLA tracker
- Pilot learning log
- If this cannot be represented in a CRM field, board, owner model, or temporary working table, it is not operational yet.

## Slide 13: Failure Modes / Anti-Patterns

- Use these anti-patterns to normalize rejection of weak work.
- Reporting meeting: Numbers reviewed without decisions -> Require decision rule and owner per item
- Vanity success: Impressions or MQLs celebrated -> Measure movement and quality
- Sales disconnected: Sales does not use content or signals -> Define sales role and weekly commitments
- Premature scale: Leadership expands before proof -> Use stop, repair, scale criteria

## Slide 14: Final Artifact

- Learners submit: Weekly cadence and 90-day pilot plan.
- ICP definition
- Buying committee map
- MOIN grid
- Content plan
- Distribution plan
- Signal-routing rules
- Weekly cadence
- Success metrics
- Anti-pattern risks
- Executive summary
- Exit gate: the artifact must change sales behavior, RevOps tracking, content decisions, or weekly cadence.

## Slide 15: Transition

- The lesson sequence ends where field implementation begins: an executive sponsor can now approve, repair, or reject the pilot from evidence.
- Next: Capstone review and field implementation.

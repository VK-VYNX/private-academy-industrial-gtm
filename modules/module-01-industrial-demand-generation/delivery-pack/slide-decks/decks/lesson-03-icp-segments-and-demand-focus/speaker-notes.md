## Instructor Use

Use these notes as the instructor depth layer. The projected slides are intentionally concise so the instructor can teach, diagnose, and run artifact production without turning the deck into a handout.

## Slide 01: ICP, Segments, And Demand Focus

- Open by naming this as 22 min of operating work, not a lecture.
- Anchor the room on the lesson output: ICP and disqualification scorecard.
- Remind participants to keep one manufacturing or export category consistent through the workshop.

## Slide 02: Lesson Promise

- Choose a narrow industrial demand focus for a 90-day pilot, define ICP triggers and exclusions, and stop wasting effort on visible but weak accounts.
- The required output is: An ICP and disqualification scorecard plus a written 90-day demand focus statement.
- Do not move on until learners know what they will submit at the end of the lesson.

## Slide 03: Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

- Industrial demand generation is resource-intensive: SME input, trusted distribution, sales context, proof, and RevOps tracking all cost effort.
- Broad TAM language turns content generic and pollutes signal routing.
- A pilot learns faster when the segment is narrow enough to produce specific questions, proof, access paths, and disqualification.
- Focus is revenue discipline, not positioning theater.
- Instructor move: ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

## Slide 04: Core Framework

- A real industrial ICP is more than firmographics.
- Sector or category
- Account type
- Trigger or timing
- Technical fit
- Commercial fit
- Buying process
- Access path
- Disqualification
- Visual companion: 04-icp-focus-and-disqualification.svg.

## Slide 05: Mental Model

- Pick the segment where the pilot can create movement.
- Pain intensity: Is the problem worth solving now?
- Trigger visibility: Can timing be detected?
- Access path: Can trusted channels reach the committee?
- Proof availability: Can evidence be built quickly enough?
- Ask the room which part would break first in their current operating system.

## Slide 06: Anonymized Industrial Example

- Composite example: Composite precision fabrication supplier.
- The company says its market is all OEMs that outsource manufacturing.
- That is too broad for demand generation and creates weak content, distribution, and routing.
- The sharper pilot ICP is export-oriented equipment OEMs needing medium-to-high complexity fabricated components with tolerance control, documentation, and repeat production potential.
- Exclude one-off fabrication jobs, no-repeat accounts, and lowest-price procurement comparisons.
- Keep the example anonymized and category-level. Do not invite named-company comparisons.

## Slide 07: Diagnostic Questions

- Which accounts are valuable enough, specific enough, and reachable enough to justify demand generation effort?
- What trigger makes the problem current?
- Which roles must be educated?
- What proof already exists?
- What account types create low-quality RFQs?
- Where does sales currently waste time?
- What segment can produce learning in 90 days?
- Use the prompts as a rapid room diagnostic before the artifact exercise.

## Slide 08: Exercise Setup

- Segment comparison and focus statement: 7 min segment scoring, 5 min focus statement.
- Compare three candidate segments by fit evidence, trigger evidence, access path, value potential, and disqualification risk.
- Choose one 90-day focus segment.
- Write the demand focus statement and at least five rejection rules.
- Expected artifact: ICP and disqualification scorecard.

## Slide 09: Group Critique

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, AI revises, humans accept or repair.
- Is the ICP narrow enough for 90 days?
- What accounts should be excluded immediately?
- What signal would prove the segment is moving?
- What buyer roles must be visible?
- What content becomes specific because the ICP is narrow?
- What will leadership be tempted to broaden too early?

## Slide 10: AI-Assisted Exercise Prompt

- Use this as the AI prompt skeleton.
- Score three candidate industrial segments by fit, trigger, access, commercial value, committee feasibility, proof availability, and pilot feasibility. Recommend one 90-day ICP, explain why it is narrow enough, define explicit disqualification rules, and list risks or assumptions humans must validate.
- Require the AI to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.
- Ranks by fit, trigger, access, value, and feasibility
- Explains why the chosen segment is narrow enough
- Includes explicit rejection rules
- Names likely committee roles and demand signals

## Slide 11: Human Correction / Quality Control

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Reject market size as the main reason.
- Reject broad categories without triggers.
- Reject segments with no commercial value threshold.
- Reject digital-access assumptions where relationship channels dominate.
- Reject any pilot without disqualification.
- Instructor move: ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality the AI could not know.

## Slide 12: RevOps Translation

- RevOps should prove that focus is real.
- ICP fit score
- Fit reason
- Trigger type
- Trigger evidence and date
- Pilot segment
- Access path
- Disqualification reason
- Progression state
- If this cannot be represented in a CRM field, board, owner model, or temporary working table, it is not operational yet.

## Slide 13: Failure Modes / Anti-Patterns

- Use these anti-patterns to normalize rejection of weak work.
- Broad market fantasy: We serve all manufacturers -> Narrow by trigger, application, value, and access
- Logo wish list: Accounts chosen because they are famous -> Require fit, trigger, proof, and access
- Price-led inbound trap: Weak-fit RFQs consume effort -> Route low-fit inquiries differently
- No exclusion logic: Every account stays in the system -> Add downgrade and disqualification rules

## Slide 14: Final Artifact

- Learners submit: ICP and disqualification scorecard.
- 90-day ICP focus statement
- Priority sector and account profile
- Trigger, technical fit, commercial fit, geography, and buying process
- Access path and buying committee assumptions
- At least five disqualification rules
- Account progression signal to measure
- Exit gate: the artifact must change sales behavior, RevOps tracking, content decisions, or weekly cadence.

## Slide 15: Transition

- A focused ICP makes the next work possible: mapping the actual questions that block movement inside that segment.

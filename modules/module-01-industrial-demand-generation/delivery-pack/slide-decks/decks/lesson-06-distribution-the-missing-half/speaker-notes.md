## Instructor Use

Use these notes as the instructor depth layer. The projected slides are intentionally concise so the instructor can teach, diagnose, and run artifact production without turning the deck into a handout.

## Slide 01: Distribution: The Missing Half

- Open by naming this as 22 min of operating work, not a lecture.
- Anchor the room on the lesson output: Distribution and ecosystem plan.
- Remind participants to keep one manufacturing or export category consistent through the workshop.

## Slide 02: Lesson Promise

- Design an industrial distribution system that gets useful insight in front of the right accounts through trusted channels, not just owned publishing.
- The required output is: A distribution and ecosystem plan with channel role, trust reason, demand state, owner, cadence, account signal, and follow-up rule.
- Do not move on until learners know what they will submit at the end of the lesson.

## Slide 03: Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

- Publishing is not distribution.
- Industrial demand moves through experts, sales, associations, exhibitions, distributors, consultants, project advisors, media, search, and referrals.
- Buyers trust field expertise and borrowed credibility more than generic company-page claims.
- Distribution must create account evidence, not just reach.
- Instructor move: ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

## Slide 04: Core Framework

- Choose channels by buyer trust and decision stage.
- Expert profiles
- Sales sharing
- Associations and export bodies
- Trade shows and technical events
- Distributors and partners
- Consultants and advisors
- Industry publications
- Website, search, and proven paid amplification
- Visual companion: 07-distribution-ecosystem.svg.

## Slide 05: Mental Model

- Dark social needs a capture rule.
- Trusted channel: A buyer, partner, consultant, or sales person mentions an account, role, problem, timing, or referral.
- Capture rule: Log source, role, demand state, strength, owner, and next action.
- Operating risk: If it stays in chat, memory, or event notes, the system undercounts real demand.
- Cadence: Review distribution evidence in the weekly account movement meeting.
- Ask the room which part would break first in their current operating system.

## Slide 06: Anonymized Industrial Example

- Composite example: Composite specialty process equipment supplier.
- Useful technical notes about repeatability and coating selection sit on the company blog.
- Buyers rarely see them before calling for price.
- The repair uses SME profile posts, sales sharing, association education, distributor proof notes, and retargeting of proven engaged accounts.
- The plan creates visibility, trust, and signals instead of simply publishing more.
- Keep the example anonymized and category-level. Do not invite named-company comparisons.

## Slide 07: Diagnostic Questions

- Where does the content need to circulate for the buyer to trust it and for the account team to detect movement?
- Which channels already have buyer trust?
- Which channels can reach the committee?
- Which channels create observable account signals?
- Where is sales already in conversation?
- Where do distributors or consultants influence decisions?
- What should be captured after distribution?
- Use the prompts as a rapid room diagnostic before the artifact exercise.

## Slide 08: Exercise Setup

- Distribution and ecosystem plan: 8 min channel plan, 4 min ecosystem map.
- Select at least five channels for the first-five assets.
- For each, define trust reason, demand state, owner, cadence, account signal, and follow-up rule.
- Map ecosystem nodes: associations, events, distributors, consultants, media, technical communities, customer peer groups.
- Expected artifact: Distribution and ecosystem plan.

## Slide 09: Group Critique

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, then the group repairs the artifact.
- Does the plan rely too heavily on owned publishing?
- Are expert profiles used where trust matters?
- Is sales distributing insight, not just asking for meetings?
- Are events designed before, during, and after the show?
- Are partners given proof and routing rules?
- Does every channel create observable account evidence?

## Slide 10: Structured Drafting Drill

- Use this as the structured drafting brief.
- Draft a channel mix for the selected ICP and first-five assets. Tie every channel to buyer trust, demand state, owner, realistic cadence, account-level signal, routing implication, and failure risk. Do not recommend more channels than the team can operate.
- Require the draft to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.
- Channel tied to buyer trust and demand state
- Owner named: sales, marketing, SME, distributor, partner, or leadership
- Realistic cadence
- Account-level signal
- Routing implication and risk

## Slide 11: Human Quality Control

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Repair channel lists that are too large to operate.
- Reject paid ads as a substitute for trust.
- Reject plans that ignore sales and partner distribution.
- Reject event plans without 72-hour routing.
- Reject company-page posting as the main motion.
- Reject distribution with no owner or cadence.
- Instructor move: ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality is missing.

## Slide 12: RevOps Translation

- Distribution creates source evidence.
- Distribution channel
- Asset circulated
- Account or cluster
- Buying role
- Demand state
- Signal strength
- Source type
- Follow-up rule
- If this cannot be represented in a CRM field, board, owner model, or temporary working table, it is not operational yet.

## Slide 13: Failure Modes / Anti-Patterns

- Use these anti-patterns to normalize rejection of weak work.
- Publishing-only: Content exists but buyers never see it -> Build channel plan and owner model
- Company-page dependency: Low reach and low trust -> Move insight through experts and sales
- Event leakage: Meetings happen but signals are not routed -> Create event signal board and 72-hour routing
- Partner noise: Distributors send weak leads -> Train ICP, proof, and routing rules

## Slide 14: Final Artifact

- Learners submit: Distribution and ecosystem plan.
- At least five channels
- Trust reason for each channel
- Demand state served
- Owner and cadence
- Account-level signal
- Follow-up rule
- One ecosystem map
- Exit gate: the artifact must change sales behavior, RevOps tracking, content decisions, or weekly cadence.

## Slide 15: Transition

- Distribution makes movement visible. The next lesson prevents the team from mistaking every visible signal for buying intent.

- Ask learners what artifact they are carrying forward before opening the next deck.

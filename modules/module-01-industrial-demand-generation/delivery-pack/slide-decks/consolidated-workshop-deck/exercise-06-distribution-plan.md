# Exercise 06 - Distribution Plan Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 26 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

This exercise turns your first-five content assets from Exercise 05 into a practical distribution and signal-capture plan. The goal is not to select every channel. The goal is to choose the few trusted paths your team can operate and follow up during the 90-day pilot.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- One distribution path for each priority asset;
- A simple trust reason for each path;
- The buyer role each path should reach;
- The expected buyer signal;
- Where that signal will be captured;
- The follow-up action;
- The owner;
- Channels to avoid for now.

The output becomes the Distribution Plan section of your Industrial Demand Generation strategy.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Copy the five priority assets from Exercise 05.
2. Choose one main distribution path for each asset.
3. Choose why the buyer would trust that path.
4. Choose the target buyer role.
5. Choose the expected buyer signal.
6. Choose where the signal will be captured.
7. Choose the follow-up action and owner.
8. List channels to avoid for now.

Use exactly five rows in the Distribution Planning Table. Each row should connect one asset to one trusted path and one follow-up action.

## Distribution Principle Quick Guide

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Trust Before Reach | Choose paths buyers believe, not only paths the company controls. |
| Buyer Fit | Match the path to the buyer role and buying stage. |
| Sales Participation | Sales should share useful insight, not only ask for meetings. |
| Field Capture | Event, partner, and distributor signals must be written down. |
| Team Capacity | Do not choose paths the team cannot operate consistently. |
| Follow-Up | Every useful signal needs a next action. |

## Distribution Path Menu

| Path | Use When |
|---|---|
| Sales Share | The asset is useful in target-account conversations. |
| Technical Expert Explanation | Expert credibility is needed. |
| Technical Webinar or Roundtable | Buyers need education and committee access. |
| Event or Exhibition Motion | Timing, project discovery, and field conversations matter. |
| Distributor or Partner Share | Local trust, referral, or market sensing matters. |
| Consultant or Advisor Enablement | Specification influence or safe recommendation matters. |
| Association or Industry Group | Borrowed industry trust is stronger than supplier promotion. |
| Website or Resource Hub | Buyers and sales need a stable proof path. |
| Search or Comparison Page | Buyers are already researching the category. |
| Paid or Targeted Promotion | The audience is narrow and the asset is already proven. |

## Trust Reason Menu

| Trust Reason | Use When |
|---|---|
| Existing Sales Relationship | The account already knows the salesperson or team. |
| Technical Credibility | Buyer needs expert judgment. |
| Industry Trust | Association, media, or event context increases credibility. |
| Partner or Distributor Proximity | Local or category access is stronger through ecosystem roles. |
| Advisor Influence | Consultants, advisors, or peer recommendations shape decisions. |
| Search Intent | Buyer is actively researching a known issue or solution category. |
| Repeated Visibility | The account needs multiple touches before action. |

## Expected Signal Menu

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Target Account Engaged | Asset reached a relevant account. |
| Buyer Role Identified | A useful buyer role became visible. |
| Buyer Question Captured | A real uncertainty surfaced. |
| Proof Gap Captured | Buyer needs evidence the team does not yet have. |
| Asset Shared Internally | Buyer enablement may be happening. |
| Partner or Distributor Flags Account | Ecosystem signal needs checking. |
| Meeting or Technical Follow-Up Requested | Sales or expert action may be needed. |
| Weak Engagement Only | Continue nurture; do not overreact. |

## Capture Method Menu

| Capture Method | Use When |
|---|---|
| Customer Relationship Management (CRM) Note | Sales owns the account or follow-up. |
| Event Signal Board | Signal comes from trade show, webinar, or field interaction. |
| Partner Signal Log | Distributor, partner, consultant, or advisor reports the signal. |
| Form or Resource Hub Data | Buyer engages through owned path. |
| Sales Call Summary | Question, role, objection, or timing appears in conversation. |
| Weekly Review Board | Signal needs team review before action. |

## Follow-Up Action Menu

| Follow-Up Action | Use When |
|---|---|
| Nurture | Fit is plausible but demand is early. |
| Research | Fit, role, trigger, or committee is unclear. |
| Map Buying Committee | One role engaged but others are invisible. |
| Enable Champion | A visible contact needs internal proof. |
| Sales Action | Fit, role, trigger, and signal are strong enough. |
| Technical Expert Follow-Up | Technical or proof issue needs expert response. |
| Partner Qualification | Ecosystem signal needs evidence before sales action. |
| Disqualify or Low-Touch | Fit is weak, timing is poor, or signal is not relevant. |

## Quality Check

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Asset Link | Every distribution path is tied to a first-five asset. |
| Trust Reason | The buyer has a credible reason to believe the path. |
| Target Role | The path is aimed at a real buying role. |
| Owner | A person or function owns the action. |
| Signal | The path can create observable account evidence. |
| Capture Method | Signal has a place to be recorded. |
| Follow-Up Action | The team knows what happens after the signal appears. |
| Exclusion Discipline | Weak or unmanageable channels are excluded. |

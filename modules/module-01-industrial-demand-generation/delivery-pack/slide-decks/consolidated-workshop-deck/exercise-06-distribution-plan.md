# Exercise 06 - Distribution Plan Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 26 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

This exercise turns your first-five content assets from Exercise 05 into a practical distribution and signal-capture plan. The goal is not to select every channel. The goal is to choose a few trusted paths the team can operate, inspect, and follow up during the 90-day pilot.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- One distribution path for each priority asset;
- Trust reason for each path;
- Demand state and target role for each path;
- Owner and cadence for each distribution action;
- Account signal to capture;
- Capture method for the signal;
- Follow-up rule after the signal appears;
- Channels to exclude for now;
- One field or ecosystem motion where relevant.

The output becomes the Distribution and Ecosystem Plan section of your Industrial Demand Generation strategy.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Copy the five priority assets from Exercise 05.
2. Choose one main distribution channel for each asset.
3. Choose the trust reason that makes the channel credible.
4. Confirm the demand state and target role.
5. Assign an owner and cadence.
6. Choose the signal the channel should create.
7. Choose where the signal will be captured.
8. Choose the follow-up rule.
9. Exclude channels that are weak, unsupported, or impossible to operate right now.
10. Add one field or ecosystem motion only if events, partners, distributors, consultants, advisors, or associations are relevant.

Use exactly five rows in the Distribution Planning Board. Each row should connect one priority asset to one trusted path and one follow-up rule.

## Distribution Principle Quick Guide

| Principle | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Trust Before Reach | Choose channels buyers believe, not only channels the company controls. |
| Demand State Fit | Match channel to Content, Solution, or Vendor Demand. |
| Sales Participation | Sales must circulate insight, not only ask for meetings. |
| Ecosystem Capture | Partner, distributor, advisor, and event signals must be logged. |
| Operational Capacity | Do not select channels the team cannot run consistently. |
| Signal Routing | Every channel should create a follow-up rule. |

## Channel Selection Menu

| Channel | Use When |
|---|---|
| Sales Share | The asset is useful in target-account conversations. |
| SME-Led Post or Explanation | Expert credibility is needed. |
| Technical Webinar or Roundtable | Buyers need education and committee access. |
| Event or Exhibition Motion | Timing, project discovery, and field signals matter. |
| Distributor or Partner Share | Local trust, referral, or market sensing matters. |
| Consultant or Advisor Enablement | Specification influence or safe recommendation matters. |
| Association or Industry Group | Borrowed trust is stronger than supplier promotion. |
| Website or Resource Hub | Buyers and sales need a stable proof path. |
| Search or Comparison Page | Buyers are already researching the category. |
| Paid or Targeted Amplification | The audience is narrow and the asset is already proven. |

## Trust Reason Menu

| Trust Reason | Use When |
|---|---|
| Existing Sales Relationship | The account already knows the salesperson or team. |
| Technical Credibility | Buyer needs expert judgment. |
| Borrowed Industry Trust | Association, media, or event context increases credibility. |
| Partner or Distributor Proximity | Local or category access is stronger through ecosystem roles. |
| Peer or Advisor Influence | Consultants, advisors, or peer recommendations shape decisions. |
| Search Intent | Buyer is actively researching a known issue or solution category. |
| Repeated Visibility | The account needs multiple touches before action. |

## Cadence Menu

| Cadence | Use When |
|---|---|
| One-Time Targeted Share | Asset supports a specific active account or meeting. |
| Weekly | Sales or SME sharing needs short-cycle repetition. |
| Biweekly | Educational content needs consistent circulation without overload. |
| Monthly | Association, partner, or newsletter motion is slower. |
| Event-Based | Distribution follows pre-event, during-event, and post-event phases. |
| Trigger-Based | Distribution happens when a signal, objection, or account movement appears. |

## Signal Captured Menu

| Signal | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Target Account Engaged | Asset reached a relevant account. |
| Role Identified | A buyer role became visible. |
| Buyer Question Captured | A real uncertainty surfaced. |
| Proof Gap Captured | Buyer needs evidence the team does not yet have. |
| Asset Shared Internally | Buyer enablement may be happening. |
| Partner or Distributor Flags Account | Ecosystem signal needs qualification. |
| Meeting or Technical Follow-Up Requested | Sales or SME action may be needed. |
| Weak Engagement Only | Continue nurture; do not over-route. |

## Capture Method Menu

| Capture Method | Use When |
|---|---|
| CRM Note | Sales owns the account or follow-up. |
| Event Signal Board | Signal comes from trade show, webinar, or field interaction. |
| Partner Signal Log | Distributor, partner, consultant, or advisor reports the signal. |
| Form or Resource Hub Data | Buyer engages through owned path. |
| Sales Call Summary | Question, role, objection, or timing appears in conversation. |
| Weekly Cadence Board | Signal needs review before routing. |

## Follow-Up Rule Menu

| Follow-Up Rule | Use When |
|---|---|
| Nurture | Fit is plausible but demand is early. |
| Research | Fit, role, trigger, or committee is unclear. |
| Map Committee | One role engaged but others are invisible. |
| Enable Champion | A visible contact needs internal proof. |
| Sales Activation | Fit, role, trigger, and signal are strong enough. |
| SME Follow-Up | Technical or proof issue needs expert response. |
| Partner Qualification | Ecosystem signal needs evidence before sales action. |
| Disqualify or Low-Touch | Fit is weak, timing is poor, or signal is not relevant. |

## Quality Check

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Asset Link | Every distribution path is tied to a first-five asset. |
| Trust Reason | The channel has a credible reason the buyer would believe it. |
| Demand State Fit | Channel matches Content, Solution, or Vendor Demand. |
| Owner | A function or person owns the channel action. |
| Cadence | The frequency is realistic for the team. |
| Signal | The channel creates observable account evidence. |
| Capture Method | Signal has a place to be recorded. |
| Follow-Up Rule | The team knows what happens after the signal appears. |
| Exclusion Discipline | Weak or unmanageable channels are excluded. |

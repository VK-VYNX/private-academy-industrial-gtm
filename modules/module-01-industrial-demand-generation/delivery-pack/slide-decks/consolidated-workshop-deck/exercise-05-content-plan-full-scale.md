# Exercise 05 - Content Plan Full-Scale Document

Use this document as the full-scale reference version of Exercise 05: Content Plan.

The short workshop worksheet is `exercise-05-content-plan.md`. This full-scale version gives deeper guidance for facilitators, participants who want more structure, and teams converting workshop outputs into an operating asset plan after the session.

## Purpose

Exercise 05 turns the top five MOIN priorities from Exercise 04 into a practical first-five content asset plan.

The goal is not to create a content calendar. The goal is to decide which assets can:

- help buyers understand risk, compare approaches, and validate supplier confidence;
- help sales act with useful context instead of generic follow-up;
- reduce buying committee uncertainty;
- create observable account signals;
- give RevOps something meaningful to track in the weekly cadence.

Industrial content is demand generation only when it changes buyer understanding, sales action, or account movement.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, the participant should have:

- five priority assets for the next 90 days;
- one clear buyer question per asset;
- demand state, buyer role, proof need, sales use, signal, and owner for each asset;
- a Build, Repair, Use As-Is, Later, or Do Not Build decision for each asset;
- a RevOps tracking structure for asset use and account movement.

## Completion Standard

Exercise 05 is complete only when each proposed asset can answer these questions:

| Question | Required Answer |
|---|---|
| Which MOIN priority does this asset serve? | A specific buyer question from Exercise 04. |
| Which demand state does it support? | Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand. |
| Which buying role needs it? | A role from the Exercise 03 buying committee map. |
| What proof makes it credible? | SME input, field evidence, technical proof, commercial logic, or named proof gap. |
| How will sales use it? | A real sales use case, not "send as collateral." |

| What signal should it create? | A meaningful account movement signal. |
| Who owns it? | A function or named owner category. |

If an asset cannot answer these questions, it is not ready for the first-five plan.

## First-Five Asset System

The first five assets should cover the minimum buyer movement system. They do not need to cover every topic. They must cover the most important buyer uncertainty across demand states and committee roles.

| Asset Type | Primary Job | Typical Demand State | Common Buyer Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Problem-Risk Asset | Make the buyer understand why the problem matters now. | Content Demand | Champion, Operations, Quality, Leadership |
| Selection Criteria Guide | Help the buyer compare approaches before defaulting to price. | Solution Demand | Technical, Procurement, Quality, Finance |
| Proof or Application Asset | Reduce technical, operational, quality, or implementation risk. | Solution or Vendor Demand | Technical, Production, Quality, Maintenance |
| Champion Internal Pack | Help the internal advocate explain the case when sales is not present. | Vendor Demand | Champion, Decision Maker, Leadership |
| Vendor Validation Asset | Prove supplier credibility, support, TCO, procurement confidence, or delivery readiness. | Vendor Demand | Procurement, Finance, Maintenance, Leadership |

## Demand State Rules

Use demand state to prevent premature vendor pitching.

| Demand State | Buyer Is Asking | Good Asset Types | Bad Default |
|---|---|---|---|
| Content Demand | What problem, risk, opportunity, or cost of inaction matters? | Diagnostic checklist, problem-risk explainer, risk guide, mistake list | Product brochure |
| Solution Demand | Which approach, requirement, implementation path, or tradeoff fits? | Selection guide, application note, comparison checklist, calculator | Generic "Why Us" page |
| Vendor Demand | Which supplier can prove value, reduce risk, support adoption, and satisfy procurement confidence? | Proof pack, validation note, TCO model, implementation plan, champion pack | Unguided sales deck |

Use Content Demand when the market is under-educated. Use Solution Demand when buyers are comparing approaches. Use Vendor Demand when supplier confidence, proof, and internal consensus matter.

## Asset Format Menu

Choose the format based on buyer uncertainty, not convenience.

| Format | Best For | Avoid When |
|---|---|---|
| Diagnostic Checklist | Helping buyers identify risk, symptoms, or gaps. | The buyer already knows the problem and needs proof. |
| Problem-Risk Explainer | Showing hidden cost, operational consequence, or urgency. | The asset becomes a product pitch. |
| Selection Criteria Guide | Helping buyers compare approaches or requirements. | Criteria are generic and not tied to the selected ICP. |
| Technical Application Note | Explaining fit, specification, performance, or integration. | SME input is unavailable or unvalidated. |
| Validation or Compliance Checklist | Quality, audit, documentation, safety, or regulated contexts. | Claims cannot be supported by evidence. |
| TCO or Cost-of-Inaction Model | Finance, leadership, or commercial justification. | Inputs are speculative or over-optimistic. |
| Implementation Roadmap | Reducing production, maintenance, training, changeover, or adoption risk. | The team cannot explain the real implementation path. |
| Supplier Comparison Worksheet | Procurement and vendor evaluation. | It turns into a lowest-price comparison. |
| Champion Internal Summary | Helping the visible contact circulate the case internally. | The buying committee is not yet mapped. |
| Buyer Enablement Pack | Combining role-specific proof for internal consensus. | The team lacks credible proof for the main roles. |

## Proof and SME Input Menu

Every serious industrial asset needs a proof source or a named proof gap.

| Proof or SME Input | Use When | Typical Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Sales Feedback | The question appears repeatedly in sales conversations. | Sales Owner |
| Technical SME Input | The asset explains fit, specification, application, performance, or integration. | Technical SME or Sales Engineer |
| Quality or Compliance SME Input | The asset touches validation, documentation, audit, safety, or inspection. | Quality or Compliance SME |
| Service or Maintenance Input | The asset explains uptime, spares, serviceability, lifecycle support, or reliability. | Service or Maintenance Lead |
| Finance or Commercial Input | The asset includes TCO, payback, cost of delay, margin, or investment logic. | Finance, Commercial, or Leadership |
| Existing Case Evidence | Prior experience exists but must be anonymized and generalized. | Sales, SME, or Marketing |
| Procurement Input | The asset supports supplier approval, terms, documentation, or comparison. | Procurement or Commercial Support |
| Proof Gap | Evidence is missing and must be repaired before publication or sales use. | Assigned by Weekly Cadence |

Common audit findings:

- Product brochures exist, but problem education is missing.
- Sales decks exist, but buyers cannot circulate them internally.
- Technical knowledge exists in SME heads, but not in reusable assets.
- Website pages exist, but proof is weak or too generic.
- Case evidence exists, but it is not anonymized or role-specific.
- Event materials exist, but no follow-up signal or routing rule exists.

## Sales Use Menu

Sales use must be specific. "Send to prospect" is not enough.

| Sales Use | Use When | Example Sales Action |
|---|---|---|
| Educate Early-Stage Account | Buyer is still learning the problem. | Share a diagnostic checklist before asking for a meeting. |
| Open Role-Specific Conversation | Sales needs a useful reason to engage a role. | Send a quality checklist to a quality head. |
| Support Technical Validation | Engineering, production, quality, or maintenance needs proof. | Use an application note in technical discovery. |
| Equip Champion Internally | Main contact must circulate the case. | Give the champion an internal summary and objection handler. |
| Handle Repeated Objection | A predictable blocker slows deals. | Send proof that addresses uptime, validation, payback, or service concern. |
| Re-Engage Stalled Account | Interest exists but movement has stopped. | Send a role-specific proof asset tied to the unresolved blocker. |
| Support Procurement or Finance Review | Commercial confidence is required. | Use a TCO model or supplier comparison worksheet. |
| Support Event Follow-Up | A field interaction created a question or objection. | Send the relevant asset within the post-event follow-up window. |

## Initial Distribution Path Menu

Exercise 06 will build the full distribution plan. Exercise 05 only needs an initial trust path.

| Path | Use When | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sales Share | Asset is useful in active or target-account conversations. | Best for role-specific enablement. |
| SME-Led Post or Explanation | Expert credibility is needed. | Best for technical or application credibility. |
| Event or Webinar Follow-Up | The asset answers a field interaction question. | Best when the question came from a real event or session. |
| Partner, Distributor, or Advisor Share | Ecosystem roles influence access or recommendation. | Best when external recommendation matters. |
| Website or Resource Hub | Buyers need an easy reference path. | Best when sales needs a stable link. |
| Association or Industry Group | Trust is stronger through an industry context. | Best for educational or standards-related content. |
| Paid or Targeted Amplification | Narrow audience targeting is required. | Use only when the ICP and asset are specific enough. |

## Signal Created Menu

Each asset should create or clarify a signal. Do not treat all engagement as buying intent.

| Signal | Meaning | Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Single View from Unknown Visitor | Possible education, but not account movement. | Weak |
| Repeat Engagement by Target Account | Learning is becoming account-level interest. | Moderate |
| Asset Shared Internally | Buyer enablement may be happening. | Moderate to Strong |
| Role-Specific Question Asked | A real committee concern is visible. | Strong |
| Technical Follow-Up Requested | Solution Demand is increasing. | Strong |
| TCO or Procurement Question Asked | Commercial evaluation is starting. | Strong |
| Multi-Role Engagement | Committee coverage is improving. | Strong |
| Sales Conversation Created | Asset created useful account action. | Strong |
| Weak Signal Only | Continue nurture; do not over-route. | Weak |

## Build, Repair, or Later Decision Menu

Use this menu to prevent unnecessary content creation.

| Decision | Use When | Next Action |
|---|---|---|
| Build Now | The asset does not exist and is needed for the 90-day pilot. | Assign owner, SME input, and draft deadline. |
| Repair | A usable asset exists but needs role, proof, sales use, or signal clarity. | Add missing role, proof, sales-use, or routing context. |
| Use As-Is | The asset is already credible, specific, and sales-usable. | Add it to the pilot asset plan and tracking structure. |
| Later | The asset is useful but not essential for the next 90 days. | Add to backlog, not the pilot. |
| Do Not Build | The asset is generic, weak-fit, unsupported, or not tied to movement. | Remove from the first-five plan. |

## Prioritization Check

If the team has more than five candidate assets, use this simple scoring check.

| Candidate Asset | MOIN Link | Proof Feasibility | Sales Use | Signal Value | 90-Day Need | Total |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
|  |  |  |  |  |  |  |

|  |  |  |  |  |  |  |

Score each field from 1 to 5.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Weak or unclear. |
| 2 | Possible but vague. |
| 3 | Useful but incomplete. |
| 4 | Strong and usable. |
| 5 | Pilot-ready. |

Do not select an asset only because it has a high total score. A score of 1 or 2 in proof feasibility, sales use, or MOIN link is a warning.

## Quality Check

Use this check before accepting the content plan.

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| MOIN Link | The asset comes from a top MOIN priority. |
| Buyer Role | The asset serves a real buying role. |
| Demand State | The asset is clearly Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand. |
| Proof | The asset has credible evidence or a named proof gap. |
| Sales Use | Sales can use it without rewriting it from scratch. |
| Distribution Readiness | The asset has at least one realistic initial path. |
| Signal Value | Engagement would produce useful account evidence. |
| ICP Fit | The asset serves the selected 90-day ICP focus. |
| Human Usability | A buyer, seller, SME, or partner could actually use it. |

Reject assets that sound polished but do not change buyer belief, sales action, or account routing.

## Failure Modes

| Failure Mode | What It Looks Like | Correction |
|---|---|---|
| Content Calendar Thinking | The plan lists topics by month. | Rebuild from MOIN priorities and buyer movement jobs. |
| Product-First Asset | The asset starts with features or claims. | Start from buyer uncertainty and proof. |
| Generic SEO Asset | The topic attracts weak-fit traffic. | Tie the asset to ICP, role, and demand state. |
| Sales-Ignored Asset | Sales cannot explain when to use it. | Add a real sales use case and account context. |
| Proof Gap Hidden | The asset makes claims without evidence. | Mark Proof Gap and assign SME repair. |
| No Signal Design | Engagement does not change routing. | Define signal created and next action. |
| Distribution Afterthought | The asset is published but not circulated. | Name an initial trust path before build. |
| Too Many Assets | The team creates a large content backlog. | Limit the pilot to first five assets. |

## Composite Industrial Example

Composite category: industrial inspection and automation-adjacent solution for plants with rising defect cost.

Selected MOIN priorities:

- Quality needs to understand why defects are escaping final inspection.
- Production needs to understand how inspection can improve without slowing throughput.
- Engineering needs to understand what technical acceptance criteria define success.
- Finance needs to understand hidden defect cost and payback logic.
- Procurement needs to compare supplier risk beyond price.

First-five content asset plan:

| Asset | Asset Type | Demand State | Buyer Role | Buyer Question | Proof or SME Input | Sales Use | Initial Distribution Path | Signal Created | Decision | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Defect-Escape Diagnostic Checklist | Problem-Risk Asset | Content Demand | Quality | Why are defects escaping final inspection? | Quality SME Input | Educate Early-Stage Account | Sales Share | Role-Specific Question Asked | Build Now | Marketing and Quality SME |
| Inspection Approach Selection Guide | Selection Criteria Guide | Solution Demand | Production | How can inspection improve without slowing throughput? | Technical SME Input | Open Role-Specific Conversation | SME-Led Explanation | Technical Follow-Up Requested | Build Now | Technical SME |
| Acceptance Criteria Template | Proof or Application Asset | Solution Demand | Engineering | What acceptance criteria define success? | Technical SME Input | Support Technical Validation | Website or Resource Hub | Technical Follow-Up Requested | Repair | Sales Engineer |
| Hidden Defect Cost Calculator | Proof or Application Asset | Solution Demand | Finance | What is the real cost of poor inspection? | Finance or Commercial Input | Support Finance Review | Sales Share | TCO Discussion Requested | Build Now | Finance and Marketing |
| Supplier Comparison Worksheet | Vendor Validation Asset | Vendor Demand | Procurement | How should suppliers be compared beyond price? | Procurement Input | Support Procurement Review | Sales Share | Asset Shared Internally | Repair | Sales and Commercial |

This plan is stronger than a calendar of posts because every asset has a buyer question, role, demand state, proof need, sales use, and signal.

## RevOps Translation

These fields or tags should be visible in CRM, the account board, content tags, or the weekly review.

| Field | What to Track |
|---|---|
| Asset Type | Problem-Risk, Selection Criteria, Proof/Application, Champion Pack, or Vendor Validation. |
| Demand State | Content, Solution, or Vendor Demand. |
| Buyer Role Served | Which buying role the asset supports. |
| MOIN Question | Buyer question answered by the asset. |
| Proof Status | Missing, Draft, Ready, or Needs SME Validation. |
| Sales Use | How sales should use the asset. |
| Signal Created | What engagement means. |
| Signal Strength | Weak, Moderate, Strong, or Very Strong. |
| Next Action | Nurture, Map Committee, Enable, Activate, Repair, or Later. |
| Owner | Person or function responsible for build, repair, or use. |

Weekly review should ask:

- Which assets created target-account engagement?
- Which roles engaged?
- Which assets created useful sales conversations?
- Which proof gaps appeared?
- Which assets should be repaired, retired, or amplified?
- Which signals should change routing?

## Final Acceptance Standard

The Exercise 05 output is accepted only if:

- all five assets come from MOIN priorities;
- every asset has a buyer role and demand state;
- every asset has proof, SME input, or a named proof gap;
- every asset has a sales use case;
- every asset has an initial distribution path;
- every asset creates a trackable signal;
- every asset has a Build, Repair, Use As-Is, Later, or Do Not Build decision;
- the plan can support the 90-day pilot without becoming a broad content backlog.

# Exercise 04 - MOIN Grid Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 19 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

MOIN means Map of Informational Needs. This exercise turns your selected ICP and buying committee map into the buyer questions, proof needs, sales uses, and signals that should guide the next 90 days.

This is not a content calendar. It is a practical map of what buyers need to understand before the account can move.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- A role-by-demand-state MOIN grid;
- Top buyer questions for the selected ICP focus;
- Proof requirements behind those questions;
- Sales use cases for each question;
- Signals that show account movement;
- Top five MOIN priorities for the next 90 days.

The output feeds Exercise 05: First-Five Content Asset Plan.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Choose five to nine role layers from the Role-Based MOIN Question Menu.
2. For each selected role, choose one question from Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand.
3. Adapt the question to your product category in plain language.
4. Use the Compact Translation Menu to identify decision risk, proof needed, sales use, and signal created.
5. Select the top five MOIN priorities that should guide the next 90 days.

Do not aim for perfect wording. Choose the closest option and make it specific enough for your market.

## Demand State Quick Guide

| Demand State | Buyer Is Asking | Asset Job |
|---|---|---|
| Content Demand | What problem, risk, opportunity, or cost of inaction matters? | Educate and Frame Urgency |
| Solution Demand | Which approach, requirement, implementation path, or tradeoff fits? | Shape Comparison Criteria |
| Vendor Demand | Which supplier can prove value, reduce risk, support adoption, and satisfy procurement confidence? | Validate Supplier Confidence |

Use Content Demand when the buyer is still learning the problem. Use Solution Demand when the buyer is comparing approaches. Use Vendor Demand when the buyer is evaluating supplier confidence.

## Role-Based MOIN Question Menu

Select the closest role layer and question. Then adapt the wording to your product, application, or buying situation.

| Role Layer | Content Demand Question | Solution Demand Question | Vendor Demand Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| Champion or Problem Owner | What problem is now painful enough to address? | Which approach can solve this without creating new internal risk? | What can I circulate internally to make this recommendation credible? |
| Technical or Engineering | What technical constraint is limiting performance? | Which requirements, specifications, or integration conditions must be clarified? | What proof shows this supplier can perform in our environment? |
| Production or Operations | Where are throughput, downtime, changeover, or operator issues showing up? | Which implementation path avoids disruption? | What proof shows implementation will not damage production stability? |
| Quality or Compliance | What defect, audit, validation, or documentation risk is increasing? | Which validation, inspection, or acceptance criteria should be used? | What proof shows this supplier can satisfy quality or compliance requirements? |
| Maintenance or Service | What recurring reliability, service, or spares issue is becoming costly? | Which support or lifecycle model reduces maintenance burden? | What proof shows spares, service, and uptime support are credible? |
| Finance or Procurement | What cost, risk, supplier, or documentation issue is not visible enough? | Which comparison criteria should be used before price is discussed? | What TCO, payback, or vendor approval proof is required? |
| Leadership or Economic Sponsor | Why does this issue deserve priority now? | Which tradeoffs should leadership understand before approving action? | Why should this supplier or approach be shortlisted now? |
| Channel, Advisor, or External Influencer | What must a recommender understand before introducing this solution? | Which qualification criteria prevent weak-fit recommendations? | What proof pack makes the recommendation safe? |

If your exact role is not listed, choose the closest role layer. For example, an EHS role usually fits Quality or Compliance; an automation role usually fits Technical or Engineering.

## Top Five MOIN Priorities

## Quality Check

Use this check before accepting the MOIN grid.

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Role Specificity | The question belongs to a real buying role. |
| Demand State Clarity | The question is clearly Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand. |
| Buyer Uncertainty | The question reflects something the buyer needs to understand. |
| Decision Risk | The question reduces a real buying or implementation risk. |
| Proof Requirement | The answer requires credible evidence, not claims. |
| Sales Use | Sales can use the answer in a real account conversation. |
| Signal Value | Engagement would reveal useful account movement. |

If the MOIN grid could become a generic blog calendar without losing meaning, it is not specific enough.

# Exercise 04 - Buyer Question Map Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 19 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

Map of Informational Needs (MOIN) means the questions buyers need answered before the account can move. This exercise turns your selected Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) focus and buying committee map into a practical buyer-question map.

This is not a content calendar. It is a map of what buyers need to understand before they can move forward.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- Five to seven important buyer roles or role layers;
- The most important buyer questions;
- The proof needed behind those questions;
- Sales use cases for those answers;
- Signals that show account movement;
- Top five buyer-question priorities for the next 90 days.

The output feeds Exercise 05: First-Five Content Asset Plan.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Choose five to seven role layers from the Buyer Question Menu.
2. Pick the closest buyer question for each role.
3. Adapt the question to your product category in plain language.
4. Choose the proof needed, sales use, and useful signal.
5. Select the top five buyer-question priorities for the next 90 days.

Do not aim for perfect wording. Choose the closest option and make it specific enough for your market.

## Demand State Quick Guide

| Demand State | Buyer Is Asking | Asset Job |
|---|---|---|
| Content Demand | What problem, risk, opportunity, or cost of inaction matters? | Educate and create urgency. |
| Solution Demand | Which approach, requirement, implementation path, or tradeoff fits? | Help compare options. |
| Vendor Demand | Which supplier can prove value, reduce risk, support adoption, and satisfy procurement confidence? | Build supplier confidence. |

## Buyer Question Menu

Select the closest question. Then adapt the wording to your product, application, or buying situation.

| Role Layer | Useful Buyer Questions |
|---|---|
| Champion or Problem Owner | What problem is now painful enough to address? / What can I share internally to make this recommendation credible? |
| Technical or Engineering | What technical constraint is limiting performance? / What proof shows this can work in our environment? |
| Production or Operations | Where are throughput, downtime, changeover, or operator issues showing up? / What proof shows implementation will not disrupt production? |
| Quality or Compliance | What defect, audit, validation, or documentation risk is increasing? / What evidence satisfies quality or compliance requirements? |
| Maintenance or Service | What recurring reliability, service, or spares issue is becoming costly? / What proof shows support and uptime are credible? |
| Finance or Procurement | What cost, supplier, or documentation issue is not visible enough? / What Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), payback, or approval proof is required? |
| Leadership or Economic Sponsor | Why does this issue deserve priority now? / Why should this approach or supplier be shortlisted? |
| Channel, Advisor, or External Influencer | What must a recommender understand before introducing this solution? / What proof pack makes the recommendation safe? |

If your exact role is not listed, choose the closest role layer. For example, a safety role usually fits Quality or Compliance; an automation role usually fits Technical or Engineering.

Priority rules:

- Prioritize questions that reduce buying committee risk.

- Do not prioritize a question only because it sounds like a good content topic.

## Quality Check

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Role Specificity | The question belongs to a real buying role. |
| Demand State Clarity | The question is clearly Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand. |
| Buyer Uncertainty | The question reflects something the buyer needs to understand. |
| Proof | The answer requires credible evidence, not claims. |
| Sales Use | Sales can use the answer in a real account conversation. |
| Signal Value | Engagement would reveal useful account movement. |

If the map could become a generic blog calendar without losing meaning, it is not specific enough.

## What to Track

| Item | What to Track |

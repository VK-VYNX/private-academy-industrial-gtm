# Exercise 04 - MOIN Grid Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 19 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

MOIN means Map of Informational Needs. This exercise turns the selected ICP focus and buying committee map into role-specific buyer questions, proof requirements, sales use cases, and account-movement signals.

You are not expected to invent a content strategy from scratch. Select the closest options, adapt the wording to your product category, and mark unknowns when evidence is missing.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- A MOIN working grid for the selected 90-day ICP focus;
- Buyer questions separated by role and demand state;
- Decision risks that explain why each question matters;
- Proof requirements that make each answer credible;
- Asset ideas that can support buyers and sales;
- Signals that show whether an account is learning, comparing, or evaluating;
- Top five MOIN priorities for the next 90 days.

The output becomes the MOIN Grid section of your Industrial Demand Generation strategy and feeds the First-Five Content Asset Plan in Exercise 05.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Choose five to nine buyer roles or role layers from your Exercise 03 committee map.
2. For each role, choose one or more demand states: Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand.
3. Select the closest buyer question from the option banks.
4. Adapt the question to your product, application, or buying situation in plain language.
5. Select one Decision Risk, one Proof Required option, one Asset Idea, one Sales Use, and one Signal Created option.
6. Complete the MOIN Working Grid.
7. Select the top five MOIN priorities for the next 90 days.

Use evidence, not optimism. If a question sounds polished but does not reduce buyer uncertainty, do not prioritize it.

## Quality Check

Use this check before accepting the MOIN grid.

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Role Specificity | The question belongs to a real buying role. |
| Demand State Clarity | The question is clearly Content Demand, Solution Demand, or Vendor Demand. |
| Buyer Uncertainty | The question reflects something the buyer needs to understand. |
| Decision Risk | The question reduces a real buying or implementation risk. |
| Proof Requirement | The answer requires credible evidence, not claims. |
| Sales Use | Sales can use the asset in a real account conversation. |
| Signal Value | Engagement would reveal useful account movement. |

If the MOIN grid could become a generic blog calendar without losing meaning, it is not specific enough.

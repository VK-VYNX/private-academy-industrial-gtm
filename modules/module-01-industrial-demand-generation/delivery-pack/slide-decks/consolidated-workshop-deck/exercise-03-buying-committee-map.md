# Exercise 03 - Buying Committee Map Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 15 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

This exercise helps you map the real buying committee for the selected ICP focus from Exercise 02. You are not expected to invent expert persona language. For each parameter, choose the option that best matches the role, then adapt the wording only where needed.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- Five to nine relevant buying roles for your selected ICP focus;
- A role-specific view of current beliefs, required beliefs, fears, blockers, and proof needs;
- A clear buyer-enablement asset for each important role or blocker;
- Three priority committee gaps to repair;
- RevOps fields that should be tracked so committee coverage becomes visible.

The output becomes the Buying Committee and Proof Map section of your Industrial Demand Generation strategy.

Do not map "the buyer" as one person. Industrial buying decisions usually require technical, operational, commercial, risk, procurement, finance, and internal-consensus proof.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Select five to nine relevant roles from the Buying Role Options.
2. Use the Default Role-Proof Matrix as a starting point.
3. For each selected role, choose one Current Belief, one Required Belief, one or two Fear or Blocker options, one Proof Needed option, one Circulatable Asset option, one Coverage Status, and one Owner.
4. If the exact option is not available, choose the closest option and write one short adaptation.
5. Mark "Unknown" when the team does not know the answer. Unknown is better than false confidence.
6. Select the three biggest committee gaps at the end of the exercise.

The goal is not a perfect org chart. The goal is to identify which roles need proof before the account can move.

| Option | Buying Role | Choose This Role When |

| R4 | Production or Operations | The decision affects throughput, uptime, changeover, operator workload, yield, or production stability. |
| R5 | Quality or Compliance | The decision affects audit readiness, validation, defect rates, documentation, inspection, safety, or customer compliance. |
| R6 | Maintenance, Reliability, or Service | The decision affects reliability, spares, troubleshooting, downtime, service load, or lifecycle support. |
| R7 | Procurement or Supply Chain | The supplier must pass commercial, documentation, vendor-approval, negotiation, risk, or terms requirements. |
| R8 | Finance or Commercial Controller | Payback, total cost, margin, cash flow, risk, or investment logic must be defensible. |
| R9 | Plant Head, General Manager, or Business Leadership | The project must compete against other plant, business, or strategic priorities. |

## Selecting the Three Priority Committee Gaps

Choose the three gaps most likely to slow or stop account movement.

Use these priority rules:

1. A role with Coverage Status CS1, CS2, or CS6 should receive attention before a role that is already enabled.
2. A role that can block technical fit, quality approval, implementation, procurement, finance, or leadership priority should not be ignored.
3. A missing proof asset is a stronger gap than a missing opinion.
4. A single-threaded account is risky even when the visible champion is enthusiastic.

## Completion Check

Before finalizing, check the map against these standards.

| Check | Pass Standard |
|---|---|
| Role Coverage | At least five relevant roles are mapped. |
| Technical and Operational Reality | Technical, production, quality, maintenance, or implementation risk is visible where relevant. |

| RevOps Visibility | Committee coverage can be inspected in CRM, the account board, or the weekly review. |

If the map only says "Owner," "Director," or "Procurement" without role-specific belief, risk, proof, and next action, it is not complete.

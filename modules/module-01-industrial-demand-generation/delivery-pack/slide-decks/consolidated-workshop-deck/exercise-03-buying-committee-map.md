# Exercise 03 - Buying Committee Map Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 15 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

This exercise helps you map the real buying committee for the selected Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) focus from Exercise 02. You are not expected to invent expert persona language. Choose the closest options, then adapt only where needed.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- Five to seven relevant buying roles;
- What each role cares about;
- What could block each role;
- What proof each role needs;
- The next action for each role;
- The top three committee gaps to repair.

The output becomes the Buying Committee and Proof Map section of your Industrial Demand Generation strategy.

Do not map "the buyer" as one person. Industrial buying decisions usually involve technical, operational, commercial, risk, procurement, finance, and leadership concerns.

## How to Complete This Exercise

1. Select five to seven roles from the Buying Role Menu.
2. For each role, choose what they care about most.
3. Choose the blocker that could slow or stop them.
4. Choose the proof that would make them more confident.
5. Choose the next action your team should take.
6. Mark the top three committee gaps.

If you do not know the answer, write "Unknown." Unknown is better than false confidence.

## Buying Role Menu

Select only roles that matter for your product category.

| Role | Choose This Role When |
|---|---|
| Champion or Problem Owner | One person feels the problem directly and may push the solution internally. |
| Decision Maker or Economic Sponsor | A senior person must approve budget, priority, investment, or timing. |
| Technical Evaluator or Engineering | The solution must fit specifications, process conditions, installation limits, or technical requirements. |
| Production or Operations | The decision affects throughput, uptime, changeover, operator workload, yield, or production stability. |
| Quality or Compliance | The decision affects audit readiness, validation, defects, documentation, inspection, safety, or customer compliance. |
| Maintenance, Reliability, or Service | The decision affects reliability, spares, troubleshooting, downtime, service load, or lifecycle support. |
| Procurement or Supply Chain | The supplier must pass commercial, documentation, vendor-approval, negotiation, risk, or terms requirements. |
| Finance or Commercial Controller | Payback, Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), cash flow, risk, or investment logic must be defensible. |
| Plant Head, General Manager, or Business Leadership | The project must compete against other plant, business, or strategic priorities. |
| External Advisor, Consultant, Distributor, or Partner | An external role can influence access, specification, recommendation, or qualification. |

Priority rules:

- A missing role is a serious gap.
- A role that can block technical fit, quality approval, implementation, procurement, finance, or leadership priority should not be ignored.
- Missing proof is stronger evidence than a missing opinion.
- A single enthusiastic contact is not enough if the wider committee is invisible.

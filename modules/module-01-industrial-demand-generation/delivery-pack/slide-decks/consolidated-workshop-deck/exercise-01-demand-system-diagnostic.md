# Exercise 01 - Demand System Diagnostic Worksheet

Use this worksheet with Slide 07 of the Industrial Demand Generation workshop.

This exercise helps you diagnose the current demand generation system for one industrial product or product category. You are not expected to invent expert language. For each layer, choose the statement that best matches your current reality.

## Exercise Outcome

By the end of this exercise, you will have:

- Six layer scores;
- The three weakest layers;
- A clear starting point for repair.

The output becomes the first section of your Industrial Demand Generation strategy.

## Working Scope

## How to Complete This Diagnostic

1. Read the five statements for each layer.
2. Choose the statement closest to your current reality.
3. Write the score in the summary table.
4. If two statements feel possible, choose the lower score unless you have current proof.
5. Mark the three lowest scores as repair priorities.

Do not search for perfect wording. The purpose is to quickly reveal what is clear, unclear, missing, or inconsistent.

## Scoring Ladder

| Score | Status | Plain Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Missing | This layer is mostly accidental or not designed. |
| 2 | Informal | The team talks about it, but it is not consistent. |
| 3 | Partly Clear | Some of it exists, but the team does not use it reliably. |
| 4 | Clear and Usable | The team can use it to guide real work. |
| 5 | Managed and Improving | The team reviews it, corrects it, and improves it. |

## Layer 1 - Market Focus

Market Focus answers: Where are we trying to create movement in the next 90 days?

| Score | Choose This Statement If It Best Matches Your Reality |
|---:|---|
| 1 | We pursue almost any company that could buy. |
| 2 | We have preferred sectors or regions, but no clear 90-day focus. |
| 3 | We have a target segment, but the trigger, urgency, use case, or exclusions are unclear. |
| 4 | We have a clear 90-day segment and can judge most work against it. |
| 5 | We actively protect focus by rejecting or downgrading weak-fit demand. |

## Layer 2 - Ideal Customer Profile and Disqualification

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) means the type of account most worth focused effort. Disqualification means knowing which accounts should not receive priority.

| Score | Choose This Statement If It Best Matches Your Reality |
|---:|---|
| 1 | We cannot reliably separate good-fit accounts from weak-fit accounts before sales time is spent. |
| 2 | Experienced salespeople know fit informally, but the logic is not visible to the wider team. |
| 3 | We have basic ICP criteria, but weak-fit accounts still consume too much effort. |
| 4 | We can judge accounts by fit, urgency, access, value, proof, timing, and service ability. |
| 5 | Fit and disqualification are actively reviewed, and the team can explain why accounts are prioritized or rejected. |

## Layer 3 - Buyer Education and Proof

Buyer Education and Proof answer: Are we helping buyers understand the problem, risk, approach, and evidence before they ask for price?

| Score | Choose This Statement If It Best Matches Your Reality |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Our materials are mostly brochures, catalog pages, exhibition posts, or price-led sales material. |
| 2 | We have some useful content, but it is scattered and not tied to buyer questions. |
| 3 | We cover important topics, but assets are not clearly tied to buyer roles or buying stage. |
| 4 | We have credible assets that answer buyer questions and help sales conversations. |
| 5 | Content is treated as a proof system and improved from buyer questions and sales feedback. |

## Layer 4 - Trusted Distribution

Trusted Distribution answers: How will useful insight reach the right industrial buyers through paths they believe?

| Score | Choose This Statement If It Best Matches Your Reality |
|---:|---|
| 1 | We publish or send materials, but there is no repeatable path to trusted buyers. |
| 2 | Sharing depends on scattered posts, event follow-up, or individual salespeople. |
| 3 | We have channels, but the target role, owner, and follow-up action are unclear. |
| 4 | We use trusted paths such as sales, experts, events, associations, distributors, partners, search, or targeted promotion. |
| 5 | We review which paths create real account movement and stop or repair weak paths. |

## Layer 5 - Signal Meaning and Next Action

Signal Meaning answers: What does buyer activity mean, and what should happen next?

| Score | Choose This Statement If It Best Matches Your Reality |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Engagement is counted, but forms, clicks, event scans, Requests for Quotation (RFQs), and conversations are treated too similarly. |
| 2 | Sales receives inquiries with limited context about fit, role, buying stage, or strength. |
| 3 | We classify some signals, but next actions are inconsistent. |
| 4 | We decide actions based on account fit, buyer role, demand state, signal strength, owner, and timing. |
| 5 | Weak signals are not overreacted to, strong signals are acted on quickly, and learning is reviewed weekly. |

## Layer 6 - Weekly Review and Ownership

Weekly Review and Ownership answer: Who reviews movement, makes decisions, and repairs the system?

| Score | Choose This Statement If It Best Matches Your Reality |
|---:|---|
| 1 | Marketing reports activity, sales follows up separately, and no one repairs the system together. |
| 2 | We have meetings or reports, but they focus mostly on activity numbers or anecdotes. |
| 3 | We review some account and pipeline information, but ownership and decisions are inconsistent. |
| 4 | A weekly review checks account movement, buyer signals, proof gaps, sales use, owners, and next actions. |
| 5 | The weekly review changes behavior: the team stops, repairs, or reinforces specific parts of the system. |

## Interpreting the Scores

| Score Range | Diagnosis | What It Means |
|---:|---|---|
| 1-2 | Needs Attention | This layer is likely blocking account movement or wasting effort. |
| 3 | Operational Gap | This layer exists, but it is not reliable enough for a serious 90-day pilot. |
| 4 | Usable Foundation | This layer can support the pilot, but still needs review. |
| 5 | Managed Strength | This layer is a strength. Protect it and use it to support weaker layers. |

Do not average away the problem. A single score of 1 or 2 can weaken the full system even if other layers look healthy.

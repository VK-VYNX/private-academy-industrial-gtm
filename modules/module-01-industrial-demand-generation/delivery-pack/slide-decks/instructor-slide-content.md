### Slide 03 - Why This Matters In Industrial Markets

Projected Content:

- Industrial reality:
  - Industrial demand often exists before it is visible in a form, RFQ, or sales call.
  - Buyers learn through consultants, distributors, exhibitions, peers, technical experts, and internal committees.
  - Lead volume can rise while high-fit account movement stays flat.
  - The operating system must connect trust, education, distribution, signal interpretation, buyer enablement, and weekly cadence.
- Instructor correction:
  1. Move from activity to account movement.
  2. Move from one persona to committee risk.
  3. Move from content volume to buyer uncertainty.
  4. Move from engagement scoring to signal interpretation.
  5. Move from reporting to weekly operating decisions.

Instructor Notes:

- Tie every point back to long-cycle risk, committee proof, and trusted circulation.
- Ask for a current example where the team is measuring activity while the account has not actually moved.

  - Which buying role is affected?
  - What would sales need before acting?
  - What would RevOps need to capture?

Instructor Notes:

- Use the critique loop: learner presents, peers name a strength, peers identify one false assumption or missing owner, then the group repairs the artifact.

### Slide 10 - Structured Drafting Drill

Projected Content:

- Title: Structure the first draft before the room critiques it.
- Brief:
  - Using the selected category, current assets, current channels, sales feedback, and CRM fields, classify the current demand motion as demand creation, demand capture, lead collection, or mixed. Identify the weakest operating layer, one practical repair that can start within two weeks, one account-level signal to track, and the validation questions that require sales, RevOps, SME, or leadership input.
- Required output standard:
  - Names the missing layer
  - Explains the anti-pattern and commercial risk
  - Defines signal, owner, and cadence
  - Lists field-validation questions

Instructor Notes:

- The brief is constrained by source evidence, assumptions, output standard, and validation.
- Require the draft to list assumptions and unknowns before learners accept any recommendation.

### Slide 11 - Human Quality Control

Projected Content:

- Title: Reject weak artifacts before they become operating practice.
- Reject or repair when the artifact:
  - Reject more-content recommendations with no distribution path.
  - Reject lead magnets before buyer trust exists.
  - Reject impressions, traffic, likes, and MQLs as primary success.
  - Reject activation before state and signal strength are known.
  - Reject invented named-company examples.
- Quality-control standard: The correction standard is not nicer wording. It is better industrial judgment: category specificity, proof, owner, field, SLA, cadence, and disqualification where needed.

Instructor Notes:

- The correction standard is operational judgment, not cosmetic polish.
- Ask what field evidence, SME proof, or sales reality is missing.

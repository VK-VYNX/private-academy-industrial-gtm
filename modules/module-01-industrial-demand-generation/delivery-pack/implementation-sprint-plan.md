# Implementation Sprint Plan

## Purpose

This plan turns Module 1 from training into a 90-day operating sprint.

Use it after a learner or client team completes the capstone.

## Sprint Governance

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| executive sponsor | approves scope, protects focus, decides stop, repair, scale |
| sales owner | validates account fit, owns activation, contributes objections |
| marketing owner | owns MOIN, content, distribution, and content performance |
| RevOps owner | owns fields, signal tracking, dashboard, and cadence evidence |
| technical or SME owner | supplies proof, application insight, and validation detail |
| AI operator | drafts, critiques, classifies, and prepares artifacts under human review |

## Phase 1: Scope And Setup

Weeks: 1-2

Objectives:

- confirm revenue problem;
- approve ICP and exclusions;
- identify target account cluster;
- define buying committee assumptions;
- configure minimum signal tracking;
- schedule weekly cadence.

Required outputs:

- pilot charter;
- account list;
- committee map;
- RevOps tracking sheet or CRM fields;
- weekly cadence invite.

Exit criteria:

- sales agrees the ICP is worth action;
- RevOps can track account, role, signal, state, owner, SLA, and next action;
- leadership accepts that early success will be measured by account movement and learning.

## Phase 2: MOIN And Content Backlog

Weeks: 3-4

Objectives:

- build MOIN grid;
- audit existing assets;
- identify proof gaps;
- prioritize first content and enablement assets;
- define sales use for each asset.

Required outputs:

- MOIN grid;
- content audit;
- first-five asset backlog;
- SME input plan;
- sales sharing notes.

Exit criteria:

- every priority asset answers a buyer question;
- every priority asset has demand state, owner, sales use, and signal design.

## Phase 3: Distribution And Signal Capture

Weeks: 5-8

Objectives:

- publish or circulate first assets;
- use expert, sales, partner, event, association, or owned channels;
- capture account-level signals;
- inspect weak and moderate signals weekly;
- refine content based on engagement and sales feedback.

Required outputs:

- distribution calendar;
- channel owner map;
- signal log;
- weekly progression notes;
- first buyer enablement gaps.

Exit criteria:

- target accounts are seeing relevant education;
- signals are captured by account and role where possible;
- sales is using content in live or warm conversations.

## Phase 4: Routing, Activation, And Buyer Enablement

Weeks: 9-11

Objectives:

- classify signals by fit, demand state, role, and strength;
- move qualified accounts into Future Pipeline or Active Focus;
- create account POVs where justified;
- build role-specific proof for committee gaps;
- enforce signal SLAs.

Required outputs:

- signal-routing review;
- Future Pipeline and Active Focus board;
- buyer enablement assets;
- opportunity review notes;
- stalled-account review.

Exit criteria:

- no strong signal lacks owner and next action;
- no account moves to Active Focus without evidence;
- buyer enablement assets address real committee blockers.

## Phase 5: Executive Readout

Weeks: 12-13

Objectives:

- summarize account movement;
- evaluate content and distribution by movement, not vanity metrics;
- inspect committee coverage;
- inspect opportunity quality;
- decide stop, repair, or scale.

Required outputs:

- executive readout;
- account progression summary;
- signal quality review;
- anti-pattern review;
- scale or repair recommendation.

Exit criteria:

- leadership can decide using observed evidence;
- benchmark assumptions are replaced with pilot data where possible;
- next 90-day motion is approved, repaired, or stopped.

## Weekly Cadence Agenda

| Agenda Item | Decision |
|---|---|
| progression wins | what moved and why? |
| Active Focus accounts | activate, enable, stall, downgrade, or disqualify? |
| Future Pipeline accounts | research, nurture, map committee, or activate? |
| new signals | route and assign owner |
| proof gaps | build asset or involve SME |
| stalled accounts | diagnose blocker |
| signal SLA | repair ownership |
| next commitments | lock owner and due date |

## Stop, Repair, Scale Criteria

| Decision | Criteria |
|---|---|
| Stop | ICP is weak, signals are low-fit, sales cannot define relevance, or value does not justify effort |
| Repair | engagement exists but account movement is weak, distribution is narrow, committee coverage is missing, or RevOps cannot inspect signals |
| Scale | target accounts move, strong signals route on time, sales uses content, committee proof gaps shrink, and qualified opportunities emerge |

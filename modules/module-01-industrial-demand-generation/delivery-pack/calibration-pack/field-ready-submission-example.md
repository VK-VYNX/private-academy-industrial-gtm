# Field-Ready Submission Example

This is an anonymized composite example. It represents the standard for supervised implementation.

## Category

Engineered pump skid systems for export-oriented process manufacturers with high downtime cost and cross-border procurement complexity.

## Executive Summary

The 90-day pilot will focus on 40 named accounts with visible expansion, modernization, or reliability triggers. The goal is not lead volume. The goal is to prove account movement: better committee coverage, clearer demand-state interpretation, higher-quality sales conversations, and qualified opportunities where technical and commercial evidence exists.

## ICP And Disqualification

| Field | Definition |
|---|---|
| account type | process manufacturers with complex fluid-handling environments |
| operating trigger | expansion, reliability failure, modernization, safety concern, or supplier-risk review |
| value threshold | enough downtime, compliance, or throughput risk to justify consultative engineering involvement |
| geography | export markets where procurement, technical proof, and service confidence affect vendor selection |
| disqualify | commodity parts need, no technical owner, low project value, no urgency trigger, no serviceability concern |

## Buying Committee

| Role | Concern | Proof Need | Enablement Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| plant engineering | fit, duty cycle, integration | application note, design checklist, drawings | send design tradeoff guide |
| operations | downtime, maintenance access, training | failure-mode guide, maintenance planning note | share downtime-risk worksheet |
| procurement | supplier risk, documentation, commercial terms | supplier qualification checklist | prepare vendor comparison pack |
| finance | business case and payback | downtime-cost model | provide business-case worksheet |
| executive sponsor | risk, timeline, strategic impact | one-page decision memo | summarize pilot-level evidence |

## MOIN Grid

| Demand State | Role | Buyer Question | Asset | Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| content demand | engineering | Why are recurring failures increasing? | failure-pattern diagnostic | repeat diagnostic use by same account |
| content demand | operations | What operational symptoms should we inspect first? | maintenance symptom checklist | checklist shared internally |
| solution demand | engineering | Which design choices reduce downtime in harsh conditions? | design tradeoff guide | technical comparison questions |
| solution demand | procurement | How do we compare supplier risk? | supplier qualification worksheet | request for qualification criteria |
| vendor demand | executive sponsor | Why should this vendor be shortlisted now? | executive decision memo | meeting request with multiple roles |

## Content And Distribution Plan

| Asset | Primary Use | Distribution | Owner | Sales Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| failure-pattern diagnostic | educate early technical buyers | expert post, sales share, association mention | marketing and SME | warm technical follow-up |
| design tradeoff guide | move solution demand | webinar, partner share, sales sequence | SME and sales | prepare discovery questions |
| supplier qualification worksheet | capture vendor demand | sales send, event follow-up, partner referral | sales and RevOps | qualify committee readiness |
| executive decision memo | enable internal sponsor | direct sales share | sales owner | support internal business case |

## Signal Routing

| Signal | Demand State | Strength | Owner | SLA | Next Action | False Positive Risk |
|---|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| single diagnostic page view | content | weak | marketing | nurture cycle | continue education | student or competitor research |
| repeated diagnostic use from target account | content | moderate | marketing and sales owner | weekly review | inspect role and topic pattern | anonymous multi-user traffic |
| design tradeoff question from engineering | solution | strong | sales and SME | 48 hours | technical discovery offer | general education request |
| qualification worksheet request | vendor | strong | sales | same day | committee and project review | procurement template collection |
| multi-role meeting request | vendor | very strong | sales leader | same day | opportunity review | unclear authority or scope |

## Weekly Cadence

| Question | Owner | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Which accounts moved state this week? | RevOps | update progression state |
| Which signals are strong enough for sales activation? | sales and RevOps | assign next action and SLA |
| Which content created useful sales conversations? | marketing and sales | keep, repair, or retire |
| Which roles are missing from active accounts? | sales | assign buyer-enablement move |
| Which false positives appeared? | RevOps | adjust routing rule |
| Which risks block pilot scale? | sponsor | stop, repair, or continue |

## Success Metrics

- account progression by demand state;
- committee role coverage;
- signal quality and false-positive rate;
- sales use of content in real conversations;
- qualified opportunity creation with evidence notes;
- cycle learning that improves routing and content.

## Anti-Pattern Controls

| Risk | Control |
|---|---|
| treating downloads as buying intent | require fit, role, state, and evidence before activation |
| broadening ICP under pressure | use disqualification rules in weekly cadence |
| content without sales use | every asset needs a sales conversation purpose |
| RevOps dashboard becomes activity reporting | inspect account progression and signal quality |
| AI invents evidence | require assumptions, unknowns, and human correction log |

Pass for supervised implementation.

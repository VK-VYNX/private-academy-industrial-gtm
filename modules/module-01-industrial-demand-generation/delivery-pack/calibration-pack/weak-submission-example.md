# Weak Submission Example

This is an anonymized composite example. It is intentionally weak.

## Category

Industrial equipment manufacturers.

## Executive Summary

We will create more awareness by posting thought leadership, running ads, and giving the sales team more leads. The goal is to increase traffic and generate more MQLs in the next quarter.

## ICP

Manufacturing companies with 50 or more employees.

## Buying Committee

- decision maker;
- influencer;
- user.

## MOIN

| Buyer Question | Content |
|---|---|
| Why do we need this? | blog post |
| What is the ROI? | ROI calculator |
| Why choose us? | case study |

## Content Plan

- 4 blog posts;
- 8 LinkedIn posts;
- 1 webinar;
- 1 downloadable guide.

## Distribution Plan

Post on social media and send to the email list.

## Signal Routing

Anyone who downloads the guide becomes an MQL and should be contacted by sales.

## Weekly Cadence

Review leads, traffic, and campaign performance.

## Success Metrics

- website visits;
- impressions;
- form fills;
- MQLs.

## Expected Decision

Reject.

## Why It Fails

- ICP is far too broad.
- Buying committee lacks role-specific concerns and proof needs.
- MOIN is a topic list, not a map of informational needs.
- Content is planned by format instead of buyer uncertainty and demand state.
- Distribution is only publishing.
- Signal routing treats a content download as buying intent.
- RevOps fields, owner, SLA, and account progression are absent.
- Metrics are mostly vanity or activity measures.

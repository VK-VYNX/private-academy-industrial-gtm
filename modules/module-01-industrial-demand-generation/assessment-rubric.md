# Assessment Rubric

Use this rubric to assess unit artifacts and the final capstone. Score each dimension from 1 to 5.

## Score Meaning

| Score | Standard |
|---:|---|
| 1 | generic, incomplete, or not operational |
| 2 | industrial language appears, but decisions are weak |
| 3 | usable draft with gaps in evidence, owner, or cadence |
| 4 | strong operating artifact with clear logic and practical next actions |
| 5 | field-ready artifact that can guide sales, marketing, RevOps, leadership, and AI agents |

## Unit Artifact Rubric

| Dimension | 1 | 3 | 5 |
|---|---|---|---|
| ICP focus | broad market | named segment | narrow ICP with triggers, fit logic, and exclusions |
| Buyer committee | generic personas | roles listed | concerns, proof needs, blockers, and owner actions mapped |
| Demand state | absent | demand states named | every content and signal decision routes by content, solution, or vendor demand |
| MOIN quality | topic list | role questions | role-specific questions tied to buyer uncertainty and required assets |
| Content utility | promotional assets | relevant assets | buyer-useful, SME-led assets with sales use and signal design |
| Distribution | publishing plan | channel list | trust-based, stage-aware distribution with cadence and owners |
| Signal routing | engagement scoring | signal categories | fit, state, role, strength, owner, SLA, next action, and false-positive risk |
| RevOps | metric list | some fields | fields, states, owners, dashboard, cadence, and decision rules |
| Buyer enablement | seller collateral | proof assets named | champion and committee enablement by role |
| AI governance | AI draft accepted | AI revised | source-grounded AI output corrected by humans |

## Capstone Scoring

| Dimension | Weight | Required For Full Credit |
|---|---:|---|
| Executive summary | 10 | clear revenue problem, scope, decision request, and pilot logic |
| ICP and disqualification | 10 | narrow category, account fit, triggers, exclusions, and value threshold |
| Buying committee | 10 | roles, concerns, proof needs, blockers, and coverage plan |
| MOIN grid | 10 | content, solution, and vendor questions by role |
| Content plan | 10 | assets mapped to buyer questions, demand states, proof, sales use, and signal |
| Distribution plan | 10 | owned, expert-led, sales, ecosystem, partner, event, paid, or search paths selected by trust and stage |
| Signal routing | 10 | rules, owners, SLAs, false positives, and opportunity criteria |
| Weekly cadence | 10 | agenda, board, owners, decision rules, and learning loop |
| Success metrics | 10 | account progression, committee coverage, signal quality, opportunity quality, and pipeline learning |
| Risk and anti-pattern control | 10 | specific failure modes and correction actions |

Passing standard: 80 or above, with no zero-score artifact. A capstone cannot pass if it lacks signal routing, weekly cadence, or disqualification logic.

## AI Output Rubric

| Dimension | Score 5 Standard |
|---|---|
| Source traceability | names source concepts or files used |
| Assumptions | separates fact, assumption, and unknown |
| Industrial translation | avoids SaaS-only or generic marketing assumptions |
| Actionability | produces fields, owners, SLA, cadence, next action, or artifact |
| Risk handling | flags blockers, false positives, and disqualification |
| Human review | asks the right correction questions |

## Certification Decision

| Decision | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Pass | artifact is ready for supervised implementation |
| Repair | artifact has promise but needs revision before field use |
| Reject | artifact is generic, unsafe, or disconnected from the operating system |
